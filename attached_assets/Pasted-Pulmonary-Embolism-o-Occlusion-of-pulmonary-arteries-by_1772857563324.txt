Pulmonary Embolism
o	Occlusion of pulmonary arteries by either blood clot, fat, air, amniotic fluid
o	Blood clot from deep veins in leg or pelvis
o	Fat: from fractured femur, hip
o	Air: from injection of large air bolus through IC of arterial line
o	Amniotic fluid: from around the time of delivery 
-	Risks:
o	Recent surgery or trauma
o	Body sends clotting factors to heal the area 
o	Inactivity, immobility
	Venous stasis occurs, blood is sluggish 
o	Pregnant
	Hypercoaguagutable state – protects from hemorrhage after birth but also greatly increases the risk for thrombus formation Clients who have cesarean births are especially at risk for thrombus formation 
o	Cesarean birth 
o	History of DVT
o	Malignancy – increased clotting factors
o	Obesity (venous pooling)
o	Oral contraceptives or hormone therapy
o	Smoking (endothelial damage)
o	Virchow triad
	Three causes of blood clot
•	Altered blood flow
•	Endothelial damage
•	Hypercoaguable state
-	Features
o	Dyspnea (most common)
o	Pleuritic chest pain
o	Hypoxemia
o	Tachypnea
o	Cough
o	Hemoptysis
o	Massive pulmonary embolism
	Feeling of impending doom
	Sudden change in mental status
	Hypotension (artery is completely obstructed)
-	Complications:
o	Pulmonary infarction (red, wedge shaped infarcts)
o	Rt sided heart failure 
o	Pulmonary hypertension
o	Obstructive shock 
-	Diagnostic studies
o	ABG
o	 D-dimer blood tests
o	CT pulmonary angiogram with IV contrast 
o	Ventilation perfusion scan 
	Normal ventilation but perfusion is decreased
o	Duplex venous ultrasound of legs to look for blood clots 
-	Nursing
o	High fowler position to help them breathe better
o	Administer:
	Anticoagulants such as heparin (IV)
	Analgesics (morphine) to decrease the workload of the heart and increase perfusion 
	Supplemental oxygen 
o	Anticipate fibrinolytics (clot busters) or embolectomy (removal of embolus) in clients with very large PE
o	Teach the client the need for long-term oral anticoagulation
	Rivaroxaban, apixaban
	Warfarin 
	If clients have hemophilia, GI bleed, etc anticoagulants and contraindicated so they may have a vena cava filter 
APTT
-	Normal on heparin is 60 seconds
-	Heparin is used in unstable angina 
-	Prevents the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin and prothrombin to thrombin 
-	Normal apt is 25-35 seconds
-	On heparin you want apt to be 1.5-2 times the normal vaue 

Epiglottitis
-	Inflammation of the tissues surrounding the epiglottis 
-	Can develop rapidly and obstruct the airway including the trachea
-	Caused by haemophilus influenza type b
-	Manifestations: sudden onset high fever, drooling, difficulty swallowing, agitation, lack of spontaneous cough, acute respiratory distress
-	Priority action is to protect the airway because it can lead to respiratory arrest and death 
-	Tripod position opens the airway and helps airflow 

Iron deficiency anemia
-	Most common chronic nutritional disorder in children
-	Many causes: premature birth, insufficient dietary intake of iron, delayed introduction of solid foods, consumption of cow’s milk before age 1, and excessive intake of cow’s milk 

Positive Pressure Ventilation
-	Pushes air into the lungs using a mechanical ventilator 
-	Can be used in acutely ill clients and can be delivered via endotracheal tube or face mask
-	Increases intrathoracic pressure during inspiration which can compress the heart and thoracic vessels
-	Reduces venous return, resulting in decreased cardiac output and hypotension
-	Hypotensive effect is greater in the presence of hypovolemia (hemorrhage, hypovolemic shock) and decreased venous tone (septic shock, neurogenic shock)
