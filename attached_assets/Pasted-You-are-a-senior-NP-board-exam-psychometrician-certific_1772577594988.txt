🇺🇸 UNITED STATES EXAMS
---------------------------------------------------------

AANP FNP
- ~150 questions (135 scored)
- 3 hours
- Clinical only
- No non-clinical theory

ANCC FNP
- ~175 questions (150 scored)
- 3.5 hours
- Clinical + professional practice

AGNP / PMHNP similar structure but specialty-weighted.

---------------------------------------------------------
US DOMAIN STRUCTURE
---------------------------------------------------------

For FNP example:

- Assessment & Diagnosis (35–40%)
- Clinical Management & Treatment (35–40%)
- Health Promotion (10–15%)
- Professional Practice (ANCC only, 5–10%)

AGNP:
- Adult/Geriatric disease management
- Chronic illness
- Palliative
- Acute episodic care

PMHNP:
- Psychiatric assessment
- Psychopharmacology
- Therapy modalities
- Crisis intervention

---------------------------------------------------------

---------------------------------------------------------

Allowed:

- Lab interpretation

- Prioritization

NOT ALLOWED:
- NGN bowtie

---------------------------------------------------------
🇨🇦 CANADA — CNPE (NEW FORMAT)
---------------------------------------------------------

The Canadian NP exam is competency-based and scenario-driven.

It emphasizes:
- Primary care management
- Clinical reasoning
- Ethics & jurisprudence
- Population health
- Indigenous health considerations
- Interprofessional collaboration

Structure:
- Case-based questions
- Short clinical scenarios
- Single best answer
- Some multi-step reasoning sequences

---------------------------------------------------------
CANADIAN DOMAIN STRUCTURE
---------------------------------------------------------

- Health Assessment & Diagnosis
- Therapeutic Management
- Health Promotion & Prevention
- Professional Role & Accountability
- System Navigation & Collaboration

Weight evenly unless official weighting differs.

STEP 2 — GENERATION RULES
---------------------------------------------------------

For each NP exam set:

Generate 300 questions per exam type.

Each set must:

• Follow domain weighting ±3%
• Include ≥ 60% case-based vignettes
• Include ≥ 20% pharmacology-heavy management
• Include ≥ 15% differential diagnosis
• Include ≥ 10% red flag/urgent referral
• Respect scope of NP practice

US:
- Use US lab units
- Include prescriptive authority logic
- Include Medicare/insurance considerations if ANCC

Canada:
- Use mmol/L
- Include Canadian prescribing frameworks
- Include cultural safety
- Include Indigenous health
- Include public system context
