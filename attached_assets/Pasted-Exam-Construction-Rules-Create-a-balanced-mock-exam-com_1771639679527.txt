Exam Construction Rules
Create a balanced mock exam composed of realistic NCLEX item types, including:
• Multiple-choice (single best answer)
• Multiple-response (select all that apply / SATA)
• Ordered response (sequencing / prioritization)
• Fill-in-the-blank (numeric calculation items)
• NGN-style items (very important), such as:
– Extended multiple-response
– Matrix/grid items
– Cloze/dropdown items
– Highlighting items
– Bow-tie questions
– Case study clusters
Distribute formats similarly to modern NCLEX exams.
Clinical Judgment Framework (Critical Constraint)
All questions must align with clinical judgment logic, including elements such as:
✔ Recognizing cues
✔ Analyzing cues
✔ Prioritizing hypotheses
✔ Generating solutions
✔ Taking action
✔ Evaluating outcomes
Questions must test decision-making, not memorization.
Question Design Requirements
Each question must:
• Be scenario-based and patient-centered
• Reflect realistic nursing practice situations
• Require interpretation or prioritization
• Emphasize safety and risk recognition
• Avoid simple definition or recall-only stems
Use common licensure-style phrasing, such as:
• “Which client requires immediate assessment?”
• “What is the nurse’s priority action?”
• “Which findings are concerning?”
• “Which intervention is most appropriate?”
Distractor Engineering Rules (Extremely Important)
Incorrect answer choices must be plausible and clinically realistic, representing common nursing errors:
• Incorrect prioritization
• Unsafe interventions
• Delayed response to deterioration
• Misinterpretation of assessment data
• Partially correct but lower priority actions
Avoid obviously incorrect or absurd options.
Answer & Rationale Requirements
For EVERY question provide:
• Correct answer(s) clearly identified
• Detailed rationale explaining clinical reasoning
• Explanation of why incorrect options are wrong
• Focus on decision-making and safety logic
Rationales must teach NCLEX-style thinking patterns.
Case Study Cluster Rules
Include NGN-style case sets where multiple questions are based on evolving patient scenarios. Require analysis of trends, clinical changes, and priority decisions.
Tone & Fidelity Constraints
Questions must feel like real licensure exam items:
• Safety-focused
• Priority-driven
• Clinically authentic
• Clear and unambiguous
• No trick wording or riddles
• No academic or textbook-style trivia
Localization Context
Use clinically neutral terminology appropriate for Canadian RN practice while maintaining NCLEX logic. Use metric units where relevant.
Output Quantity
