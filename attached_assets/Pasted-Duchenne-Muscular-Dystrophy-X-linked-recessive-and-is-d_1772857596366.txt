Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
-	X-linked recessive and is due to a lack of a protein called dystrophin needed for muscle stabilization
-	Disease onset is 2-5 years 
-	Muscles of the proximal lower extremities and pelvis are affected first
-	Calf muscles hypertrophy initially in response to proximal muscle weakness and are later replaced by fat and connective tissue
-	Gower sign: involves the use of one’s hands to rise from a squat or from a chair to compensate for proximal muscle weakness
-	There is no cure. Most are wheelchair bound by adolescence and die by age 20-30 from respiratory failure 
-	It is important to avoid clutter (rugs) and prevent falls
-	Clients are encouraged to participate in regular gentle recreation based exercises and swimming 

Femoral Cardiac Catheterization
-	Risk for rapid, life threatening hemorrhage after removal
-	Immediately following cardiac catheterization the HOB should be less than 30 degrees to prevent hip flexion which could disrupt clot formation at the insertion site and initiate bleeding 

Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy
-	Symptoms:
o	Lower abdominal pain and pelvic pain, amennorhea, possibly followed by vaginal spotting or bleeding, and a palpable adnexal mass on pelvic examination. 
o	May rupture, leading to hemorrhage
o	Hypotension, tachycardia, dizziness, referred shoulder pain (from irritation of the diaphragm by intraabdominal blood) a ruptured ectopic pregnancy is a surgical emergency and requires immediate intervention 

Radiation Therapy
-	Protect the skin from the sun during and after treatment
-	Cleanse skin daily using lukewarm water and mild soap
-	Protect the skin from infection and trauma (loose fitting clothing)

Subdural Hematoma
-	Bleeding in the subdural space and is the result of blunt force head trauma
-	Life threatening, increased pressure from the hematoma on the brain can lead to decreased cerebral perfusion and hernation (mid-line shift). Assessing for signs of increased intracranial pressure, including ALOC, cushing triad (hypertension, bradycardia, and irregular respirations), ipsilateral puil dilation, headache, and vomiting, is critical as surgery to evacuate the hematoma and relieve pressure may be necessary

Percutaneous Kidney Biopsy
-	Inserting needle through skin to obtain a renal tissue sample that is used to determine the cause of certain kidney diseases
-	Bleeding from the site is a major complication because the kidney is highly vascular
-	Hypertension increases renal arterial pressure, which increases the risk for postprocedure bleeding
-	Blood pressure must be lowered and well-controlled before performing a kidney biopsy 
-	Also watch for clotting disorders and thrombocytopenia (decreased platelets) 

Knee Arthroplasty
-	Average hospital length is 3-5 days 
-	Immediate PT is necessary
-	Isometric quadricepts setting is initiated on the first postop day 
-	Client should be fully weight bearing by discharge 
-	Clients use an assistive device to help them sit, rise safely from a sitting to a standing position, and to negotiate steps 
-	Knee immobilizer is used to maintain extension during ambulation and at rest for about 4 weeks 

Course Crackles
-	Loud, low pitched bubbling
-	Heard during inspiration and not cleared by coughing
-	Sounds like Velcro being pulled apart
-	Present when fluid or mucus collects in the lower respiratory tract (pulmonary edema, pulmonary fibrosis) 
-	Lasix should be used to treat

Asthma
-	Chronic condition characterized by inflammation, swelling, narrowing of the airways in the lungs
-	Client will experience chest tightness, wheezing, uncontrollable coughing, rapid respirations, retractions, and anxiety and panic 
-	Treatment includes nebulized breathing treatments with a short acting beta-agonist medication such as albuterol, oral or IV corticosteroids 
-	O2 saturation  is the best indicator of treatment effectiveness as it reflects gas exchange 

Cervical Cerclage
-	Suture placed to keep the internal cervical os closed, restrict cervical dilation, and prevent preterm birth (birth <37 weeks age)
-	The client should report symptoms of preterm labor (low back pain, discomfort, regular contractions that continue to intensify, pelvic pressure) because they indicate that further intervention may be required to prevent preterm birth (tocolysis, corticosteroids, magnesium sulfate)

Acute Blood Loss
-	Medical emergency
-	Can lead to hemodynamic instability
-	Nurse should lower the head of the bed, and place client in the supine position with the feet elevated to maintain perfusion to the brain and other organs

Active TB tuberculosis
-	Treated with combination drug therapy
-	Isnoniazid causes hepatotoxicity and peripheral neuropathy
-	Rifampin also causes hepatotoxicity
-	Baseline liver function tests should be obtained
-	Patients should watch for hepatotoxicity: jaundice, anorexia 
-	Ethambutol causes ocular toxicity and clients will need frequent eye exams 
-	Rifampin: 
o	Changes the colour of body fluids due to wide distribution
o	Tears can turn red, making contact lenses discoloured
o	Clients should wear eyeglasses 
o	Women should use nonhormonal birth control
