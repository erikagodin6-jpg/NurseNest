RATIONALE CONTENT FORMAT

Example structure:

Correct Answer
Monitoring distal peripheral pulses and hourly urine output

Why This Is Correct
After AAA repair the greatest risk is graft occlusion or impaired perfusion. Monitoring distal pulses ensures blood flow to the lower extremities while urine output confirms renal perfusion.

Key Exam Concept
Post-operative vascular surgery priorities focus on circulation and organ perfusion.

Why Other Options Are Incorrect

A Assessing bowel sounds
GI function is not the immediate priority following vascular graft surgery.

C Early ambulation
Ambulation is encouraged later but does not address immediate graft perfusion concerns.

D Measuring abdominal girth
Abdominal girth monitoring does not directly assess vascular graft patency.

Clinical Pearl

After major vascular surgery always assess:

• distal pulses
• urine output
• limb perfusion

These are early indicators of graft complications.
