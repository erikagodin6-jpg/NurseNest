Prompt 1: Master Lesson Generator (Deep Patho, No Cole’s Notes)
Copy/paste and replace the bracketed fields.
Create a highly comprehensive nursing lesson page on: [DISEASE/CONDITION].

Audience: RPN, RN, NP learners. This is for a paid education platform. Write like a premium clinical reference, not a quick study sheet.

NON-NEGOTIABLE DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- Pathophysiology must be a deep mechanistic explanation at the cellular/tissue/organ level.
- Include inflammatory cascades, receptor/ligand pathways, cytokines/mediators, hemodynamic and neurohormonal compensations, and progression over time (early → late disease).
- Explain “why” for every major sign/symptom and lab abnormality.
- Avoid vague phrases like “causes inflammation” without naming mediators and the chain of events.
- No oversimplified bullet lists. Use paragraphs with clear subheadings.

FORMAT (use these exact headings)

1. Introduction
- Definition, epidemiology (high-level), typical clinical contexts, why it matters.

2. Pathophysiology Deep Dive (Mechanism-Level)
- Start with the initiating trigger/insult.
- Explain cellular responses (injury, hypoxia, oxidative stress, endothelial dysfunction, immune activation).
- Describe key mediators (e.g., IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α, histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, complement, coagulation pathways where relevant).
- Describe microcirculatory changes (capillary leak, vasodilation/vasoconstriction, shunting, edema).
- Describe organ-level consequences and compensatory mechanisms (SNS, RAAS, ADH, HPA axis, ventilation/perfusion shifts, Starling forces).
- Timeline: early vs late findings and how the physiology evolves.
- Include 1–2 short “mechanism maps” written in text (A → B → C → D).

3. Signs and Symptoms With Mechanisms
For each major sign/symptom:
- What it is
- Why it happens physiologically (mechanism)
- What it suggests clinically (severity/phase)

4. Labs and Diagnostics (Disease-Specific)
Include:
- Expected lab abnormalities (direction + why)
- Key diagnostic tests and what they show
- Important differentials that can mimic the labs
- Red-flag thresholds that indicate danger

5. Nursing Assessment
- Focused history questions and risk factors
- Physical assessment priorities and what each finding means mechanistically
- Monitoring plan (vitals, I&O, trends)

6. Nursing Interventions (RPN/RN/NP separated by scope)
For each intervention:
- Action + rationale tied to pathophysiology
- Monitoring parameters and adverse effects
- Escalation criteria

7. Priorities and Emergencies
- ABCs and time-critical actions
- Deterioration patterns
- When to call rapid response / provider
- Immediate vs ongoing priorities

8. Complications
- Mechanism of each complication
- Early warning signs
- Prevention/mitigation

9. Patient & Family Teaching
- Explain in plain language without losing accuracy
- Warning signs requiring urgent care
- Medications, adherence, lifestyle considerations

10. Clinical Pearls & Common Exam Traps
- High-yield distinctions
- Common mistakes
- “If you only remember 5 things” (short list allowed here only)

QUALITY CHECK (MANDATORY)
At the end, include a short checklist confirming:
- At least 800–1,200 words total
- At least 10 explicit “why” explanations
- At least 8 named mediators/pathways (if applicable)
- No generic filler
If any requirement is not met, revise before outputting.
