SECTION 4 — LANGUAGE-SPECIFIC QUALITY RULES
==================================================

General rules for all languages:
- do not translate the brand name NurseNest
- preserve nursing abbreviations where appropriate:
  RN, RPN, NP, NCLEX, NICU, PICU, ICU
- preserve clinical accuracy
- avoid awkward machine-translated phrasing
- maintain a professional, modern, supportive tone
- do not break internal terminology consistency

Portuguese rules:
- use Brazilian Portuguese, not European Portuguese
- keep healthcare/nursing terms natural for Brazilian users

Tagalog rules:
- use natural Filipino phrasing
- do not over-literalize English copy
- keep CTAs clear and trust-building

Hindi rules:
- use clean, readable modern Hindi
- keep technical medical terms understandable
- preserve internationally recognized nursing abbreviations when appropriate
- avoid overly formal or awkward textbook phrasing
