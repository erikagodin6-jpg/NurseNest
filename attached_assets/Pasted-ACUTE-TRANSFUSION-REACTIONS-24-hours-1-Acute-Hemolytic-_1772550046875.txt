8. Hypotensive Transfusion Reaction
Mechanism: Bradykinin accumulation (often associated with ACE inhibitors)
Key feature: Isolated sudden hypotension during transfusion
Important exam pearl: No hemolysis, no fever. BP improves after stopping transfusion.
9. Acute Pain Transfusion Reaction
Rare but recognized.
Severe chest/back pain shortly after transfusion begins without hemolysis or hypotension.
10. Non-Immune Hemolysis
Mechanical or thermal destruction of RBCs due to:
Improper warming
Hypotonic IV solutions
Small-bore tubing
11. Air Embolism (very rare)
Due to improper administration technique.
🟡 DELAYED TRANSFUSION REACTIONS (> 24 hours to weeks)
These are commonly tested in advanced nursing and NP-level content.
12. Delayed Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction (DHTR)
Mechanism: Anamnestic IgG response to minor RBC antigens
Timing: 3–14 days post transfusion
Signs: Mild jaundice, falling hemoglobin, positive Coombs
Exam trap: Patient seemed fine initially.
13. Delayed Serologic Transfusion Reaction
New antibody detected post-transfusion without clinical hemolysis.
14. Post-Transfusion Purpura (PTP)
Rare immune reaction
Severe thrombocytopenia 5–10 days later
Risk of bleeding
Seen more often in multiparous women
15. Transfusion-Associated Graft-Versus-Host Disease (TA-GVHD)
Rare but fatal
Donor T-cells attack recipient
Rash, diarrhea, pancytopenia
Occurs 4–30 days post transfusion
Prevented with irradiated blood products.
16. Iron Overload (Hemosiderosis)
Chronic transfusion complication
Seen in:
Thalassemia
Sickle cell disease
Iron deposits in liver, heart, endocrine organs.
17. Transfusion-Transmitted Infections
Though rare due to screening:
Hepatitis B/C
HIV
CMV
West Nile virus
Parvovirus B19
🔵 METABOLIC / ELECTROLYTE COMPLICATIONS
These are especially relevant in critical care and massive transfusion protocols.
18. Hyperkalemia
From stored RBC potassium leakage.
19. Hypocalcemia
Citrate toxicity binds calcium.
20. Hypothermia
From rapid infusion of cold blood.
21. Dilutional Coagulopathy
Massive transfusion dilutes platelets and clotting factors.
