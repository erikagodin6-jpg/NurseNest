PART 2: CAT QUESTION -> FLASHCARDS
Convert CAT questions into flashcards where educationally appropriate.

Do not make weak low-value flashcards.
The flashcards must feel intentional, exam-focused, and clinically useful.

For each CAT question, generate 1 to 3 flashcards depending on the question complexity.

FLASHCARD TYPES TO GENERATE
1. Concept flashcard
- front: high-yield question or prompt testing the underlying concept
- back: concise answer with exam-focused explanation

2. Priority / recognition flashcard
- front: what finding/action/assessment is priority in this scenario?
- back: correct priority plus short why

3. Clinical cue flashcard
- front: key symptom, lab, assessment, or risk cue
- back: what it suggests / what action it connects to

4. Pharmacology flashcard when applicable
- front: medication, side effect, contraindication, teaching, antidote, or nursing implication
- back: concise exam-focused answer

5. NGN clinical judgment flashcard when applicable
- front: identify the most important cue / condition / action / parameter
- back: concise reasoning

FLASHCARD QUALITY RULES
- no bloated paragraphs on the card front
- back should be concise but high yield
- preserve nursing exam language
- keep cards suitable for spaced repetition
- avoid simply copying the full CAT stem unless necessary
- make flashcards feel like memory anchors, not mini essays
- if a question is too context-heavy, extract the core learning point into a cleaner card

EXAMPLE
If CAT question asks about heparin complications:
Possible flashcards:
- front: Which findings suggest bleeding complications in a client receiving heparin?
  back: Petechiae, bleeding gums, melena, and hematuria suggest anticoagulant-related bleeding and require prompt follow-up.
- front: What is a key nursing priority when a client on heparin shows black tarry stools?
  back: Suspect GI bleeding and notify the provider promptly while assessing hemodynamic stability.
- front: Heparin therapy: what clinical finding is most concerning?
  back: Unexpected bleeding such as hematuria, melena, petechiae, or mucosal bleeding.
