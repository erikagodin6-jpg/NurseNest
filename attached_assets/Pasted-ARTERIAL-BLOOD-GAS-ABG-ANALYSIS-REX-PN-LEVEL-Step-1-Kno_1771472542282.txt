ARTERIAL BLOOD GAS (ABG) ANALYSIS – REX-PN LEVEL
✅ Step 1 – Know Normal Ranges (Memorization Required)
Component	Normal Range	What It Reflects
pH	7.35 – 7.45	Overall acid–base status
PaCO₂	35 – 45 mmHg	Respiratory (acid)
HCO₃⁻	22 – 26 mEq/L	Metabolic (base)
PaO₂	~80 – 100 mmHg	Oxygenation (contextual)
Exam-safe memory anchor:
⭐ pH = balance
⭐ CO₂ = acid (lungs)
⭐ HCO₃⁻ = base (kidneys)

✅ Step 2 – Identify Acidosis vs Alkalosis
First question always:
Is pH low or high?

• pH < 7.35 → Acidosis
• pH > 7.45 → Alkalosis

Do NOT start with CO₂.

✅ Step 3 – Determine Respiratory vs Metabolic Cause
Match pH direction with culprit:
🫁 Respiratory Problem → Look at PaCO₂
CO₂ = acid
Pattern	Meaning
↑ CO₂ → ↓ pH	Respiratory acidosis
↓ CO₂ → ↑ pH	Respiratory alkalosis
🧪 Metabolic Problem → Look at HCO₃⁻
Bicarbonate = base
Pattern	Meaning
↓ HCO₃⁻ → ↓ pH	Metabolic acidosis
↑ HCO₃⁻ → ↑ pH	Metabolic alkalosis
🧠 HIGH-YIELD ABG PATTERNS FOR REX-PN
Focus on common clinical scenarios.
✅ Respiratory Acidosis (VERY COMMON)
Cause logic:
⭐ Hypoventilation → CO₂ retention

Seen in:

• COPD
• CNS depression (opioids, sedatives)
• Respiratory failure

Recognition themes:

🚨 Drowsiness
🚨 Confusion
🚨 ↓ LOC

✅ Respiratory Alkalosis
Cause logic:
⭐ Hyperventilation → CO₂ loss

Seen in:

• Anxiety
• Pain
• Early hypoxia

✅ Metabolic Acidosis (VERY HIGH YIELD)
Seen in:
• Diarrhea (bicarbonate loss)
• DKA
• Renal failure

Recognition themes:

🚨 Compensation = rapid breathing

✅ Metabolic Alkalosis
Seen in:
• Vomiting
• NG suction

Mechanism:

Loss of acid

🚨 CRITICAL EXAM SAFETY RULES
⭐ Compensation ≠ Correction
If pH abnormal → problem still exists
⭐ Respiratory Problems Change Fast
Metabolic changes slower
⭐ Always Link ABGs to Clinical Picture
Exam trap:
❌ Correct numbers, wrong interpretation

Example:

High CO₂ + COPD + drowsy = danger

🧪 HIGH-YIELD LAB RESULTS FOR REX-PN
REX-PN heavily tests electrolytes & perfusion labs.
✅ Potassium (EXTREMELY IMPORTANT)
Condition	Danger
Hyperkalemia	Dysrhythmias
Hypokalemia	Weakness + arrhythmias
Common causes:
• Diuretics
• Vomiting / diarrhea
• Renal failure

✅ Sodium (VERY HIGH YIELD)
Brain-sensitive electrolyte.
Condition	Major Risk
Hyponatremia	Confusion / seizures
Hypernatremia	Neuro changes
✅ Glucose (CRITICAL)
🚨 Hypoglycemia = Immediate danger
Symptoms:
• Sweating
• Tremor
• Confusion

🚨 Hyperglycemia
Symptoms:
• Polyuria
• Dehydration

✅ Creatinine / Renal Labs
Reflect kidney function.
Implications:

🚨 Medication dosing
🚨 Fluid balance

✅ Hemoglobin / Hematocrit
Low → Oxygen delivery issue
Recognition themes:

• Fatigue
• Tachycardia

✅ White Blood Cell Count (WBC)
Elevated → Infection suspicion
Interpret WITH symptoms.

🚨 COMMON LAB INTERPRETATION EXAM TRAPS
❌ Treating numbers without context
❌ Missing medication causes
❌ Ignoring trends
❌ Ignoring symptoms
✅ SAFE MEMORY ANCHORS FOR STUDENTS
ABGs
pH → Acid vs base
CO₂ → Respiratory acid
HCO₃⁻ → Metabolic base

Electrolytes
⭐ Potassium → Heart
⭐ Sodium → Brain
⭐ Calcium → Muscle / neuro

Danger Signals
• Sudden LOC change
• Dysrhythmia risk labs
• Severe glucose changes
