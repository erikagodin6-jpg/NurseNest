Morning Sickness
-	Characterized by nausea with or without vomiting, commonly occurs during the first trimester due to rising hormone levels of estrogen, progesterone, and hcg. 
-	Teaching:
o	Consume dry, high carb foods
o	Low fat proteins
o	Eat several small meals throughout the day
o	Drink fluids between and not with meals
o	Consume drinks or foods with ginger
o	Eat foods high in vitamin b6 (nuts, seeds, legumes)

Pleurisy
-	Characterized by stabbing chest pain that increases on inspiration or with cough 
-	Caused by inflammation of the visceral pleura and the parietal pleura 
-	Pleural friction rub is auscultated in the lateral lung fields over the area of inflammation. The sound is produced by the 2 layers rubbing together and can indicate pleurisy, a complication of pneumonia. It is characterized by squeaking or crackling

Testicular Torsion
-	Emergency in which blood flow to the testis has stopped. The testicle rotates and twists the spermatic cord, initially causing venous drainage obstruction that leads to swelling and severe pain. Arterial blood supply is interrupted, resulting in testicular ischemia and necrosis of the testis 
-	There is a short time frame in which it can be identified with ultrasonography and treated, generally 4-6 hours, making this condition a priority

ACE inhibitors
-	Decrease ventricular remodeling in chronic heart failure
-	Reduce circulating aldosterone, promote vasodilation, increase urine production
-	Prevent the breakdown of bradykinin which can induce a persistent nonproductive cough 
-	Adverse effects: fatigue, dizziness, mood changes, hyperkalemia, rarely angioedema
-	If patient is unable to tolerate ACE inhibitors, valsartan can be prescribed 
-	Common adverse effects of ARBs are respiratory infections, headache, dizziness, insomnia, GI upset, fatigue

Septic Arthritis
-	Joint infection that involves the hip or knee in young children
-	Most often cause by the hematogenous spread of bacteria from elsewhere in the body
-	Manifestations: fever, joint pain, refusal to bear weight 
-	Children prefer to keep the affected hip externally elevated to relieve pressure on the inflamed joint capsule
-	Immediate evaluation and treatment are required because delays can result in permanent joint destruction 
-	Treatment incudes antibiotics and surgical drainage to decompress the joint space and remove inflammatory debris 


IUD
-	Copper IUD is a long acting reversible contraception that causes intrauterine inflammation and impairs sperm mobility and prevents implantation of a fertilized egg
-	Causes mild discomfort, cramping, light vaginal bleeding on insertion
-	Ibuprofen is recommended before and after insertion 
-	Menstrual changes are common, heavier bleeding and increased cramping during menses are expected
-	Has immediate effect
-	IUD strings should be checked at least every month 

Defibrillator
-	Synchronized cardioversion is indicated for ventricular tachycardia with a pulse, SVT, and atrial fibrillation with a rapid ventricular response
-	If the synchronize function is not turned on the unit defibrillates without sensing the patients rhythm so the shock may be delivered during a vulnerable time (T-wave) and can result in a more lethal rhythm 
-	Synchronize must be turned on when cardioversion is planned because it is programmed to sense the client’s rhythm and deliver a shock on the R wave in the QRS comple

Misoprostol (prostaglandin E1)
-	Cervical ripening agent
-	Can stimulate frequent contractions and is contraindicated in patients with
o	Abnormal fetal heart rate patterns – indicates decreased fetal oxygenation 
o	Uterine tachysystole (>5 contractions in 10 minutes)
o	Previous cesarean birth as it increases the risk of uterine rupture
o	History of renal failure
-	Should be administered at least 4 hours after the administration of uterotonic medications to prevent uterine rupture 



Increased ICP in Infants
-	Findings:
o	Prominent scalp veins, wide-spaced suture lines, bulging anterior fontanel 
o	Can be more prominent when babies cry and can becaused by hydrocephalus or menintitis 
o	High pitched, shrill cry and difficulty with feeding coordination
o	Cardiopulmonary distress, seizures, somnolence
o	Continuous downward gaze with protrusion of the frontal area of the head (setting sun sign) is an ophthalmic finding indicating that periaqueductal structures are compressed from increased ICP, impairing upward gaze
o	Increased head circumference

Hydrocephalus in children
-	Causes:
o	Impaired csf circulation, impaired csf absorption, excessive csf production
-	Manifestations: head examination 
o	Rapidly enlarging head circumference and or macrocephaly
o	Prominent scalp veins
o	Full anterior fontanelle
o	Behavioural changes and or developmental delay
o	Lower extremity weakness and spacticity
o	Signs of increased ICP: headache, vomiting, papilledema, impaired upward gaze
o	Hypertension, bradycardia
-	Evaluation: neuroimaging (ultrasonography if fontanelle is open), CT scan for signs of acutely increased ICP
