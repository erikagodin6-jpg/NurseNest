Wound Irrigation
-	Flushing out debris and bacteria from an open wound to reduce risk of infection to promote healing
-	Important for wounds sustained in an outdoor environment 
-	Contamination with dirt increases the risk for infection 
-	Tetanus grows in the dirt 
-	Determine tetanus vaccination status 
o	Steps:
	Start with hand hygiene 
	Assess for drainage and pain
	Provide pain medication 30 mins before wound irrigation
	Ensure a towel or absorbant pad is placed under 
	Remove old dressing
	Position wound to allow gravitational flow of solution
	Flow should work with gravity
	Open sterile supplies and put on gloves 
	Select a 35 ml sterile syringe with a large bore angiocatheter for perfect pressure and fill with irrigating solution
	Hold tip of syringe 1 inch above the wound to prevent the syringe from contaminating the wound 
	Flush with low continuous pressure 
	Irrigate down from the top edge of the wound
	Flush until the solution is free from debris and exudate
	Dry with sterile gauze
	Apply dressing
	Time date and initial dressing 


Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation
-	Life threatening disorder when there is widespread activation of coagulation cascade
-	Consumption of clotting factors and platelets  abnormal clot 
-	Causes:
o	Trauma – MVC, surgery 
	Body sends clots to clean the wound and they will be used up 
o	Sepsis/infection – body recognizes bacteria as toxins and tries to get rid of them
o	Stress 
o	Obstetric – placental abruption, preeclampsia due to strained vessels, retained dead fetus 
o	Malignancy – procoagulation factors are released which will summon clotting factors and use them all up and none are left for the rest of the body 
-	Manifestations
o	Internal manifestations will show first due to microthrombi that obstruct blood flow to kidneys
	Renal insufficiency, hematuria, decreased urine output
	External bleeding
•	Gums, iv sites
	Internal bleeding (petechiae, ecchymosis) 
-	Lab findings
o	Decreased platelet count, fibrinogen level
o	Increased PR/INR, activated PTT, d-dimer 
-	Treatment
o	Supportive
	Treat underlying cause 
	Frequently assess for bleeding in urine or at iv sites 
	Monitor platelets, pt, ptt
	Replace clotting factors and FFP 
	Replace platelets
	PRBC
	Administer oxygen as necessary
	Monitor urine output and maintain >30ml/hour

Cerebral Edema
-	Excessive fluid in the brain 
-	Caused by: increased CSF, obstruction, or impaired reabsorption
o	Head trauma, surgery, masses, tumors, infecton, encephalopathy, vascular dysfunction
-	Features
o	Increased intracranial pressure
o	Headache, vomiting
o	Change in LOC
o	Posturing: 
	Decorticate (hands curled in)
	Decerebrate (hands curled out)
o	Hyperthermia
o	Restlessness
o	Unilateral dilated pupil (CN3 compression)
o	Fixed bilateral pupils (brain stem herniation)
o	Cushing triad: hypertension with widening pulse pressure, bradycardia, irregular respirations (usually slow)
o	Late sign: brain herniation – medical emergency
-	Nursing
o	Maintain calm, nonstimulating environment
o	HOB 30 degrees to facilitate drainage
o	Head in a neutral position
o	Monitor ICP, vital signs, neurological status
o	Prpare for ETT 
o	Administer hypertonic saline (fluid enters vascular space and leaves the brain tissue)
o	Mannitol 
o	Stool softener 
o	Muscle relaxer

-	Causes antibiotic associated colitis 
-	Antibiotic use  altered normal healthy gut flor  colonization and overgrowth of C diff  produces toxin and spores
-	Toxin causes current symptoms
-	Spores survive and recur for months
-	Spores from diarrhea can survive on skin and clothes for months

o	Recent or prolonged abx use
o	Age >65
o	Current or recent hospitalization or LTC stay
o	Use of PPI or immunosuppressants

Esophageal Varices
-	Varices – distended tortuous fragile blood vessel
-	Veins drain blood from body and go to heart to be reoxygenated
-	Blood from esophagus goes into portal vein, when there is cirrhosis the liver tissue enlarges and obstructs the blood into the portal vein = portal hypertension = blood backing up into stomach veins and leads to varices
-	Pressure builds and becomes more distended and fragile and can rupture which leads to life threatening upper GI bleeds
-	Cirrhosis patients may not know they have esophageal varices 
-	Asymptomatic without rupture
-	Diagnosed during endoscopy
-	Manifestations of hemorrhage
o	Vomiting bright red blood or clots 
o	Can mimic an arterial bleed
o	Risk for aspiration 
o	Coffee ground emesis or melena
o	Monitor for hypovolemic shock 
	Tachycardia, paleness, hypotension
-	Nursing
o	NPO 
o	Protect airway 
o	Elevate HOB
o	Patient may need to be intubated 
o	Implement interventions that prevent hypovolemic shock 
o	IV fluids 
o	Bolus and continuous
o	PRBC to maintain BP and improve perfusion
o	Octreotide infusion (vasoconstriction around esophagus to control bleeding)
o	PPI (panto)
o	Prepare for EGD 
	Sclerotherapy (meds to control bleeding)
	Band ligation
o	Blakemore tube
	Like an NG but goes through oropharynx
	Inflated to stop bleeding

Borderline Parsonality Disorders
-	Manipulative behaviour that is aimed at gaining control and power over a person or situation for personal gratification 
-	Often use staff splitting or flattery
-	Clients may use false information to tell on a staff member or act in a way that gives the impression of sincerity and caring towards staff members for personal gain 
-	Nursing interventions:
o	Setting limits that are realistic, nonpunitive, and enforceable
o	Using a nonthreatening, firm tone when discussing limits and the consequences of unacceptable behaviours

Separation Anxiety
-	Occurs from 6-30 months
-	There are three stages
o	Protest: child refuses attention from others, screams for the parent to return, and cries unconsolably
o	Despair: child is withdrawn, quiet, uninterested in activities or meals, and displays younger behaviour 
o	Detachment: child suddenly appears happy and interested in building relationships 
-	Nurses must build trust and encourage connection with family and familiar environments even when they are absent
-	Interventions: 
o	Encourage the parents to leave favourite toys, books, pictures from home
o	Establishing a daily schedule that is similar to the child’s home routine
o	Maintaining a close, calming presence when the child is visibly upset
o	Facilitating phone or video calls when parents are available
o	Providing opportunities for the child to play and participate in activities

Methotrexate
-	Folate antimetabolite, antineoplastic, immunosuppressant drug used to treat various malignancies and as a nonbiologic disease modifying antirheumatic drug used to treat rheumatoid arthritis and psoriasis
-	Can cause bone marrow suppression resulting in anemia, leukopenia, and thrombocytopenia 
-	Leukopenia and its immunosuppressant effects can increase susceptibility to infection
-	Clients should be educated about obtaining routine killed vaccines and avoiding crowds and persons with known infections 
-	Alcohol should be avoided as it is hepatotoxic and drinking alcohol increases the risk for hepatotoxicity

Cervical Cancer
-	Malignancy of the cervix occurs near the meeting point of the vaginal and uterine epithelia located in the endocervical cancal
-	Uterine epithelial cells in the area are rapidly and constantly replaced with squamous cells, a natural process that also increases the risk for abnormal cell changes and cancer
-	The most significant risk factor is persistent infection with HPV, a common and often asymptomatic sexually transmitted infection that is identified in almost all clients with cervical cancer
-	Other risk factors are related to the likelihood of acquiring hpv ability to clear infections, or factors that increase the cancer-causing effects of HPV infection, including
o	History of sexually transmitted infections
o	Engaging in sexual partners
o	Early childbearing
o	Smoking tobacco
o	Immunosuppression 
o	Long term use of oral contraceptives
-	A patient who gave birth at age 16 (sexually active at an early age, early childbearing) and has had multiple sexual partners has three risk factors for cervical cancer

Acute postinfectious glomerulonephritis
-	Occurs when immune complexes are deposited in the glomeruli following with certain strains of bacteria 
-	Patients have decreased renal blood flow and glomerular filtration, which causes excessive sodium and water retention leading to hypertension and edema
-	Treatment includes sodium and fluid restriction to decrease circulating fluid volume and reduce edema 
-	Hypotonic solutions can worsen edema in clients with apgn and lead to life threatening pulmonary or cerebral edema

Vaginal hematoma
-	Abnormal pooling of blood within the vaginal wall space 
-	During delivery stretching of the vaginal canal can rupture blood vessels particularly in nulliparous clients who had an operative vaginal birth 
-	Clients often have massive occult bleeding within the vaginal wall, resulting in minimal visible bleeding and a protruding vaginal mass
-	This is often associated with rectal or vaginal pain or pressure 
-	Uterine findings remain normal (fundus is firm and midline)

Hepatitis
-	Inflammation of the liver caused by infection, toxins, trauma resulting in impairment of liver function (bile production, detoxification of blood, metabolism). Nursing interventions for clients with acute viral hepatitis include: 
o	Rest: 
	Alternate periods of rest and activity to reduce metabolic demands and avoid fatigue
	Avoid hepatotoxins like alcohol and Tylenol as they worsen injury to liver cells
	Medications metabolized in the liver should be used cautiously to allow hepatocytes to heal 
o	Nutrition:
	Encourage low fat, small, frequent meals to decrease nausea and promote intake in clients with anorexia 
	Anorexia is lowest in the morning so promote eating a larger breakfast
	Provide oral care and avoid extremes in food temperature to increase appetite 
	Promote water consumption and diets adequate in carbs and calories
o	Infection Control
	Hepatitis b is transmitted through sexual contact and infected blood, condoms should be used and clients should not share razors or toothbrushes

Phototherapy
-	Light is absorbed by the skin and converts bilirubin to water-soluble form, allowing it to be excreted in the stool and urine
-	Client should be fully exposed, except for diaper and eye shields, when placed under the phototherapy lights
-	Maintaining skin integrity is important because the breakdown of bilirubin can cause frequent stools and produce skin excoriation and breakdown 
-	However lotions and ointments should not be applied because they can absorb heat an dcause burns 

Rhabdomyolisis
-	Muscle tissue is damaged and myoglobin is released into the blood due to the rapid breakdown of muscle cells 
-	Common causes.= muscle injury due to overexertion, crush injuries, drug use, certain medications like statins
-	Clients with manifestations of rhabdomyolysis (dark urine, muscle pain, markedly elevated creatinine kinase) require immediate follow up because myoglobin can be nephrotoxic in large quantitites and cause aki 
-	Treatment – large volumes of IV fluids to dilure myoglobin in the blood and protet the kidneys from further damage 

Chronic Kidney Disease
-	Risk for fluid overload and hyperkalemia
-	Clients should avoid salt substitutes which typically contain potassium chloride and may contribute to hyperkalemia 
-	To avoid further complications and prevent progressive kidney damage, clients with CKD are advised to follow certain dietary restrictions including
o	Sodium restriction: avoid high sodium foods such as cured meats, pickled foods, canned soups, frankfurters, cold cuts, soy sauce, and salad dressings
o	Potassium restriction: avoid high potassium foods such as raw carrots, tomatoes and orange juice
o	Avoid bran, peanut butter, raisins, saltines, milk 
o	Fluid intake monitoring: include all foods including popsicles and gelatin
o	Low protein diet: unless on hemo, where they should increase protein intake
o	Low phosphorus diet: avoid foods high in phosphorus (chicken, turkey, dairy)

Anaphylaxis
-	Immune mediated hypersensitivity reaction to an allergen that can rapidly progress to respiratory failure and cardiovascular collapse.
-	Caused by release of histamine and other mediators such as nitric oxide, tryptase 
-	Most concerning findings:
o	Swelling of the lips and tongue (angioedema) which can rapidly progress to laryngeal edema and upper airway obstruction
o	Bilateral wheezing, caused by air movement through a narrow airway and is indicative of bronchoconstriction
o	Wheezing in the presence of labored breathing required immediate intervention to open the airway
o	Hypotension which is caused by vasodilation and increased capillary permeability which can lead to anaphylactic shock
-	Mediators result in urticaria due to localized vasodilation, severe bronchoconstriction, angioedema, upper airway obstruction, abdominal discomfort, hypotension
-	First line treatment is epinephrine and the preferred route is im.
-	Repeat every 5-15 mins if symptoms persist
-	After epinephrine, administer corticosteroids like methylprednisolone to decrease airway inflammation and bronchodilators to dilate the small airways 
-	Supportive measures include oxygen, fluid bolus, corticosteroids, bronchodilators
-	Frequrently assess for stridor (high pitched, grating noise that indicates obstructed upper airway airflow and laryngeal edema)
-	Request a prescription for an antihistamine to reduce urticaria and itching
-	Ensure intubation equipment is ready 
-	Document the allergy
-	Client is at high risk for aspiration and choking so patient should be npo
-	Anticipate observing the patient for 4-6 hours following resolution of symptoms because anaphylaxis can resolve and come back up to 36 hours after onset (biphasic reaction)

Rheumatoid Arthritis
-	Chronic autoimmune disorder characterized by inflammation and damage to synovial joints 
-	This usually involves the wrists and proximal small joints of the fingers 
-	Progressive fibrosis of joint membranes results in pain, deformities, swan neck, ulnar deviation, and stiffness especially in the morning. Over time, remodeling of joint capsules and associated pain reduce the ability to perform activities of daily living and engage in routine tasks 
-	Disease modifying antirheumatic drugs improve long-term joint function and slow the progression of bony erosions and rheumatoid nodules 
-	Methotrexate helps inhibit the growth of rapidly dividing inflammatory cells and is the preferred disease modifying treatment for most clients 
-	Nsads and systemic glucocorticoids (prednisone) may also be prescribed for symptom management and pain control

Atopic Dermatitis
-	Chronic skin disorder characterized by pruritis, erythema, and dry skin
-	The exact cause is unknown, although it may be associated with an impaired skin barrier and resulting immune response to invading allergens
-	The primary goals of management are to alleviate pruritis and to keep skin hydrated to reduce scratching. Scratching can lead to excoriations and secondary infections
-	Teaching includes:
o	Bathing with tepid water and mild/gentle soap, hot water and long bubble baths should be avoided
o	Patting skin gently after bathing, followed by immediate application of topical emollient to seal in moisture 
o	Giving an oral antihistamine at bedtime to releve pruritis which tends to be worse at night
o	Keep childs nails trimmed short 
o	Dress in soft clothing like cotton 
o	Long sleeves should be worn at night

Azathioprine

-	Fatigue and nausea can be expected as minor adverse effects that may be associated with the disease
-	Leukopenia can be a severe adverse effect and should be reported to the hcp immediately

HIV
-	Viral infection of CD4 cells that results in progressive immune system impairment
-	Most frequently transmitted via blood, uprotected sexual contact, shared needles, and parenteral equipment, or perinatally from mother to child
-	Primary prevention and odification of risky behaviours are essential health care strategies for HIV because there is no known cure
-	Preexposure prophylaxis is a preventative strategy in which antiretroviral therapy is prescribed for clients whose risk for contracting HIV is high 
-	Emtricitabine/tenofir is a commonly used combination therapy for prep in high risk individuals 

Infant Formula
-	Readily available as ready to feed, concentrate, or powder
-	Keep bottles, nipples, caps and other parts as clean as possible 
-	Wash the tops of formula cans with hot water and soap prior to opening to prevent contamination
-	Refrigerate any unused prepared formula or unused opened formula but use within 24 hours or discard to reduce the risk of bacterial growth
-	Warm bottles in a pan of hot water or under warm tap water for several minutes nut not in the microwave
-	Test formula temperature on the inner wrist before serving to the infant 
-	Never overdilute or overconcentrate 
-	Discard any formula left over because the saliva mixed with it fosters bacterial growth 
-	Discard prepared formula after 24 hours

Kidney Transplant
-	Concerning findings following kidney transplant:
o	Decreased urinary output which can indicate dehydeation, obstruction, or impaired kidney perfusion
o	Fever, chills, malaise, arthralgia, and abdominal tenderness (at right iliac region) indicating potential infection or organ rejection
o	Signs of fluid retention like pitting edema in lower extremities, hypertension indicating impaired GFR and kidney dysfunction
-	Rejection:
o	Onset is usually less than 6 months
o	Exposure to donor antigens induces activation of t lymphocytes predominantly cell mediated
o	Asymptomatic with increase in BUN and creatinine on routine laboratory examination
o	Pain/tenderness of the transplanted kidney
o	Symptoms of acute kidney injury (edema, dyspnea)
o	Treatments include increasing immunosuppressants and or dose
o	Rejection is reversible with the appropriate treatment 
	Anticipate immunosuppressants and high dose corticosteroids to reduce the inflammatory response and minimize damage to the donated organ. Steroids can be tapered down as rejection resolves 
	Prepare for dialysis until the transplanted kidney regains function

-	Tramadol can lead to serotonin syndrome when taken with ssri

o	Mental status changes (anxiety, restlessness, agitation) 
o	Autonomic dysregulation (diaphoresis, tachycardia, hypertension, hyperthemia)
o	Neuromuscular hyperactivity
-	Treatment: 
o	Discontinue serotonergic agents and administer a benzodiazepine to improve agitation and decrease muscle contraction (clonus) which reduces temperature

Severe Burns
-	Priority is to ensure the airway is secure
-	Nursing:
o	IV analgesics
o	Tetanus immunization as burn wounds are prone to tetanus
o	Foley catheter to monitor urine output as genital burns cause edema and urinary retention
o	Cleansing and debriding wounds – avoid skin disinfectants like chlorhexidine and poviodine based cleaning 
	Use saline water or normal saline 
o	Collect blood for carboxyhemoglobin level 
o	Burn injury is a hypermetabolic process and clients need early supplemental nutrition so enteral nutrition is associated with multiple clinical benefits and preferred over IV nutrition
-	Wound injuries:
o	Administer IV analgesics
o	Assess for drug allergies
o	Gather sterile supplies
o	Heat room to 85 
-	Signs of infection
o	Temperature instability 
o	Progressive tachycardia
o	Refractory hypotension
o	Stress induced hyperglycemia
o	New onset feeding intolerance like increased gastric residual may reflect abdominal hypoperfusion 

Osteomyelitis
-	Bone infection that occurs in pediatric clients due to hematogenous spread of bacteria through the bloodstream into the well-vascularized metaphysis of long bones (tibia)
-	Inflammation compromises blood flow and forces the infectious exudate into cortical and periosteal bone causing necrosis 
-	Eventually the areas of dead bone separate from living bone, forming sequestra
-	Manifestations:
o	Fever, pain, erythema, swelling
-	Without treatment, sepsis can develop. Obtain a blood culture and sensitivity to determine the causative pathogen and administer antibiotics

o	Urinary retention

	Can cause paralytic ileus

Aortic Dissection
-	Tear in the inner lining of the aorta that allows blood to surge between the layers of the arterial wall, separating and weakening the aortic wall
-	Perfusion to vital organs may become impaired and the dissection can rapidly progress to life-threatening complications
-	Surgical repair is required
-	Before surgical repair it is priority to decrease the risk for rupture by maintaining normal pressure in the aorta so you should give IV beta blocker therapy to reduce blood pressure and heart rate

Chronic Kidney Disease
-	Patients frequently develop anemia due to decreased erythropoietin production by the kidneys and clients are typically treated with recombinant human erythropoietin to alleviate the symptoms of anemia by stimulating cells in bone marrow to produce more red blood cells 
-	Erythropoietin therapy increases the risk for hypertension, stroke, MI, blood clots, and heart failure due to thickened blood related to the higher concentration of RBCs
-	Uncontrolled hypertension is a contraindication to therapy so the nurse should check the clients BP before administering

Myopia

Hemodialysis
-	Involves filtering blood in a closed system outside the body for the treatment of CKD
-	Blood is passed through a semipermeable membrane with dialysis solution (dialysate) which creates an osmotic gradient that filters blood, removes excess fluid and waste and corrects electrolyte imbalances
-	Air embolism is a life threatening complication of hemodialysis that occurs when air enters the blood stream through the hemodialysis catheter
-	The air embolism displaces blood in the pulmonary vessels and causes dyspnea, chest pain, severe anxiety, changes in sensorium, hypotension, reduced oxygen saturation 
-	The nurse should immediately clamp the access tubing to prevent additional air from entering circulation and should apply oxygen to relieve dyspnea

-	Caused by transfusion of mismatched blood

-	Occurs seconds to minutes after initiation of transfusion due to massive histamine release

Febrile nonhemolytic transfusion reaction
-	Most common
-	Benign occurrence caused by cytokine release from leukocytes within the donor blood product. Clinical manifestations include fever, chills, and malaise within 1-6 hours of transfusion initiation 
-	No skin changes, hypotension, angioedema, or dark red urine
