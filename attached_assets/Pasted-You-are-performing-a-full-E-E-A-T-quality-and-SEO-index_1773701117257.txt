You are performing a full **E-E-A-T quality and SEO indexing audit** for a healthcare education website similar to NurseNest.

The website provides study guides, certification preparation, and exam practice content for nursing students and healthcare professionals.

Because this is medical and educational content, it falls under **Google YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)** categories and must meet the highest standards of **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (E-E-A-T)**.

Your goal is to ensure every page on the site is considered **high-quality, authoritative, and indexable by Google**.

The site currently contains approximately **319 public pages**.

You must audit all pages and recommend improvements where necessary.

---

GLOBAL QUALITY STANDARDS

Ensure every page meets the following standards:

1. Demonstrates **real clinical expertise** appropriate for nursing education.
2. Contains **accurate, medically sound explanations**.
3. Avoids thin, generic, or superficial explanations.
4. Uses structured headings and logical educational flow.
5. Provides actionable learning value for nursing students.
6. Includes clear internal navigation and contextual links.
7. Contains unique content not duplicated elsewhere on the site.

If content appears thin or overly generic, expand it with additional clinical depth.

---

CONTENT QUALITY REVIEW

Evaluate each page and confirm the following elements exist.

The page must include:

• a clear educational objective
• a concise introduction explaining the topic
• structured educational sections

Examples of sections for clinical topics:

• definition
• pathophysiology
• signs and symptoms
• diagnostic tests and labs
• medications and pharmacology considerations
• nursing interventions
• patient monitoring
• complications
• exam tips or clinical pearls
• practice questions when applicable

Ensure explanations include **clinical reasoning and nursing rationale**, not only definitions.

---

EXPERIENCE AND EXPERTISE SIGNALS

Each medical page must clearly demonstrate professional credibility.

Ensure the following are present:

• a visible author or reviewer section
• a statement such as:
“Medically reviewed by a Registered Nurse”

• author credentials where applicable (RN, NP, etc.)

Include a short reviewer bio if possible.

This is required for healthcare content to meet Google E-E-A-T expectations.

---

MEDICAL REFERENCES

Add references where appropriate using reputable sources such as:

World Health Organization
Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
National Institutes of Health
peer-reviewed medical journals
recognized clinical guidelines

References should be formatted in a consistent citation style.

---

STRUCTURE AND SEO OPTIMIZATION

Every page must contain the following elements.

SEO TITLE
• unique and descriptive
• includes primary keyword

META DESCRIPTION
• concise summary
• encourages click-through

H1 HEADING
• matches the primary topic

LOGICAL CONTENT STRUCTURE
• H2 and H3 headings organize the material

INTERNAL LINKS
• link to 3–8 related educational pages

These internal links should support a **topic cluster structure**.

---

INTERNAL LINKING AND TOPICAL AUTHORITY

Ensure the site forms a strong **knowledge network**.

For example:

A page on heart failure should link to:

• pulmonary edema
• ACE inhibitors
• diuretics
• heart failure nursing interventions
• related practice questions

This signals topical authority to search engines.

---

USER EXPERIENCE AND PERFORMANCE

Verify the following:

• pages load quickly
• mobile layout is responsive
• navigation is clear
• page layout is readable
• no broken images or formatting errors

Ensure content appears immediately in the HTML and is not hidden behind client-side rendering that search engines cannot access.

---

STRUCTURED DATA

Implement structured data where appropriate.

Examples:

Article schema for study guides
FAQ schema for question sections
Breadcrumb schema for navigation

Structured data should follow schema.org standards.

---

SEO INDEXING AUDIT

Perform a full crawlability and indexing check.

Tasks:

1. Generate a complete XML sitemap containing every public page.

2. Ensure the sitemap is available at:

/sitemap.xml

3. Verify robots.txt allows search engine crawling and does not block important pages.

4. Confirm no public pages contain a “noindex” directive.

5. Ensure canonical tags point to the correct primary URL.

6. Identify orphan pages that are not linked internally.

7. Ensure each page has a unique title and meta description.

8. Confirm server responses:

• valid pages return HTTP 200
• missing pages return HTTP 404

9. Ensure JavaScript rendering does not prevent Google from seeing page content.

10. Confirm the sitemap is submitted in Google Search Console.

---

CRAWL DEPTH

Ensure every page can be reached within **three clicks or fewer** from the homepage.

If pages are deeper in the architecture, create additional internal links.

---

OUTPUT REPORT

Produce a structured report that includes:

1. Pages fully compliant with quality and SEO standards
2. Pages needing content expansion
3. Pages missing internal links
4. Pages missing metadata
5. Pages blocked from indexing
6. Orphan pages
7. Pages missing structured data
8. Technical SEO issues detected

For each page requiring improvement, provide **specific recommended enhancements**.

Do not delete pages.

Improve and expand them so that all ~319 pages become **high-quality, authoritative, and fully indexable educational resources** suitable for ranking in Google search results.
