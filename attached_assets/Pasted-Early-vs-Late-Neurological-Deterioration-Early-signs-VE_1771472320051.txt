Early vs Late Neurological Deterioration
Early signs (VERY testable):
🚨 Restlessness
🚨 Confusion
🚨 Headache
🚨 Subtle LOC change
Late signs:
🚨 Decreased LOC
🚨 Posturing
🚨 Pupillary changes
🚨 Respiratory compromise
Exam trap: waiting for dramatic signs
✅ Level of Consciousness (LOC) Changes
Core exam logic:
Sudden LOC change = urgent assessment priority
Applies across all ages.
✅ Perfusion & Oxygenation Link
Brain highly sensitive to:
• Hypoxia
• Hypotension
• Glucose imbalance
Many neuro questions are secondary physiology questions.
👶 PEDIATRIC NEUROLOGICAL PATHOLOGY (High Yield)
Pediatric neuro questions focus on recognition & safety, not rare syndromes.
✅ Seizures (EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD)
Must know:
• Febrile seizures
• Post-ictal state
• Safety priorities
• Airway protection
Critical exam themes:
🚨 First action = safety
🚨 Not restraining
✅ Increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP)
Recognition patterns:
• Bulging fontanelle (infants)
• Irritability
• Vomiting
• Altered LOC
Late ominous signs:
🚨 Bradycardia
🚨 Irregular respirations
✅ Meningitis (VERY HIGH YIELD)
Classic exam condition.
Recognition:
🚨 Fever
🚨 Neck stiffness
🚨 Lethargy
🚨 Photophobia (older child)
Priority logic:
Early recognition + isolation precautions
✅ Hydrocephalus (Conceptual Only)
Recognition:
• Enlarging head circumference
• ICP signs
✅ Head Injury / Concussion
Very exam-friendly.
Red flags:
🚨 Vomiting
🚨 Worsening headache
🚨 LOC change
🧑 ADULT NEUROLOGICAL PATHOLOGY (Core Exam Domain)
✅ Stroke (VERY HIGH YIELD / TOP PRIORITY)
Absolute must-know.
Recognition:
⭐ Sudden deficits
⭐ FAST principles
⭐ Ischemic vs hemorrhagic (basic)
Priorities:
🚨 Airway
🚨 Swallow safety
🚨 Time sensitivity
✅ Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)
Core concept:
Temporary deficits ≠ benign
✅ Seizure Disorders
Know:
• Triggers
• Safety
• Post-ictal logic
✅ Increased ICP
Recognition:
• Headache
• Vomiting
• LOC changes
✅ Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
Exam themes:
• Early deterioration
• Neurological checks
• Safety priorities
✅ Spinal Cord Injury (Simplified)
Know:
• Above vs below injury implications
• Autonomic issues (basic concept)
✅ Multiple Sclerosis (Conceptual Recognition)
Patterns:
• Relapsing symptoms
• Neuro deficits
✅ Parkinson’s Disease
Functional & safety focus:
• Rigidity
• Tremor
• Fall risk
👵 GERIATRIC NEUROLOGICAL PATHOLOGY (VERY IMPORTANT)
Questions often combine neuro + safety + cognition.
✅ Delirium (EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD)
Must differentiate from dementia.
Core recognition:
🚨 Acute onset
🚨 Fluctuating LOC
🚨 Often reversible cause
✅ Dementia (VERY HIGH YIELD)
Recognition:
• Gradual decline
• Memory impairment
• Safety implications
✅ Alzheimer’s Disease
No deep pathology — focus on:
• Cognitive decline
• Behavioral changes
• Care priorities
✅ Parkinson’s Disease
High frequency in exam scenarios.
• Mobility
• Dysphagia risk
• Medication timing concepts
✅ Stroke (Still Very Common)
Same priorities as adult logic.
✅ Falls & Head Injury
Very testable geriatric pattern.
Red flags:
🚨 Anticoagulant use + fall
🚨 Delayed neuro symptoms
🧠 HIGH-YIELD SYMPTOM-BASED EXAM PRESENTATIONS
REX-PN often tests patterns, not diagnoses.
Students must recognize:
🚨 Neurological Red Flags
• Sudden weakness
• Sudden LOC change
• New confusion
• Severe headache
• Seizure activity
🚨 ICP Danger Signs
• Decreasing LOC
• Vomiting
• Pupillary changes
• Bradycardia (late)
💊 MEDICATION & SAFETY CONCEPTS (Major Exam Focus)
✅ Anticonvulsants (General Logic)
• Sedation risk
• Safety monitoring
✅ Parkinson’s Medications (Basic Awareness)
• Timing importance
• Mobility implications
✅ Sedatives / CNS Depressants
Exam themes:
🚨 Fall risk
🚨 Confusion risk
❌ COMMON STUDENT STUDY ERRORS
Students over-study:
❌ Rare neuro syndromes
❌ Detailed neuroanatomy
❌ Complex lesion localization
Low yield.
