Exam Focus Areas
How Anatomy & Physiology Appears on Nursing Exams
A&P isn't tested in isolation — it's embedded in clinical reasoning questions across every exam tier.

RPN / LPN (REX-PN, NCLEX-PN)
Focuses on foundational anatomy: cardiac output basics, airway anatomy for suctioning, GI tract for tube feeding placement, and renal anatomy for fluid balance questions.

RN (NCLEX-RN)
Tests deeper pathophysiology connections: hemodynamic changes in heart failure, ventilation-perfusion mismatch, neurological assessment correlating to brain anatomy, and electrolyte shifts across body systems.

NP (AANP, ANCC, FNP-BC)
Requires advanced integration: differential diagnosis based on anatomical structures, pharmacological mechanisms tied to receptor physiology, and system-level pathophysiology for treatment planning.
