Early vs Late Hypoxia Signs (VERY HIGH YIELD)
REX-PN strongly tests early recognition.
Early Hypoxia
• Restlessness
• Anxiety
• Tachycardia
• Tachypnea
Late Hypoxia
• Cyanosis
• Bradycardia
• Decreased LOC
• Respiratory failure signs
🚨 Waiting for cyanosis = exam error
✅ Work of Breathing Recognition
Identify distress patterns:
• Accessory muscle use
• Nasal flaring
• Retractions
• Inability to speak full sentences
✅ Breath Sound Interpretation (Simplified)
Not auscultation mastery — just basic meaning.
Sound	Likely Meaning
Crackles	Fluid / congestion
Wheeze	Airway narrowing
Diminished	Poor air entry
Absent	Emergency potential
🫁 HIGH-YIELD RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS
Focus on common + exam-friendly disorders.
✅ COPD (EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD)
Must know:
• CO₂ retention logic
• Hypoxic drive concept (basic)
• O₂ titration caution
Recognition priorities:
🚨 Increasing drowsiness
🚨 Confusion
🚨 Worsening dyspnea
Exam traps:
❌ Over-oxygenation logic
❌ Misreading SpO₂
✅ Asthma
Mechanism:
Bronchoconstriction → Airflow limitation
Key findings:
• Wheezing
• Dyspnea
• Chest tightness
🚨 Silent chest = danger sign
✅ Pneumonia
Mechanism:
Alveolar inflammation / consolidation
Key findings:
• Fever
• Crackles
• Hypoxia
• Productive cough
Priorities:
Oxygenation + infection recognition
✅ Pulmonary Embolism (VERY IMPORTANT)
Classic exam pattern.
Recognition:
🚨 Sudden dyspnea
🚨 Tachycardia
🚨 Chest pain
🚨 Hypoxia
✅ Atelectasis
Mechanism:
Alveolar collapse
Associated with:
• Post-operative patients
• Immobility
Prevention logic frequently tested.
✅ Respiratory Failure (Recognition Only)
Danger signs:
• Altered LOC
• Severe hypoxia
• Exhaustion
🫁 OXYGEN THERAPY & SAFETY (Major Exam Domain)
REX-PN loves oxygen safety questions.
✅ Oxygen Is a Medication
Must know:
• Requires order (jurisdiction dependent)
• Monitor response
• Titrate appropriately
✅ Delivery Device Logic (Basic Only)
Understand relative differences:
• Nasal cannula → low-flow support
• Mask systems → higher concentrations
No need for exact FiO₂ memorization.
✅ Critical Oxygenation Errors
❌ Ignoring increasing oxygen needs
❌ Treating SpO₂ in isolation
❌ Missing clinical distress signs
🫁 ACID-BASE LINK (High-Yield Simplification)
Respiratory involvement:
Hypoventilation → Respiratory acidosis
Hyperventilation → Respiratory alkalosis
Only conceptual understanding needed.
🫁 PRIORITY & DETERIORATION LOGIC (CRUCIAL)
Students pass when they recognize instability early.
🚨 Findings Requiring Attention
• Sudden dyspnea
• Increased work of breathing
• Altered LOC
• Falling SpO₂ with symptoms
• Absent breath sounds
❌ COMMON EXAM TRAPS
❌ Waiting for cyanosis
❌ Over-focusing on numbers
❌ Ignoring mental status changes
❌ Confusing anxiety vs hypoxia
❌ Missing COPD oxygen considerations
