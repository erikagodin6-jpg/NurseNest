Ventriculoperitoneal Shunt
-	Initiate seizure precautions
-	Shave the hair on the infants head
-	Mark the location where the head circumference was measured
-	Verify the patient’s understanding of the indication, risks, alternatives, and right to refuse the procedure 
-	Interventions after insertion:
o	Assess for abdominal tenderness because the baby is at risk for ileus, constipation, and peritonitis 
o	Report a fever
o	Test the surgical site for presence of glucose (spinal fluid contains glucose)
-	Teaching:
o	Shunt requires revision surgeries as the child grows or if the shunt malfunctions
o	Certain sports like boxing and wrestling should be avoided when the child is older due to risk for shunt displacement or disconnection
o	Early childhood development programs can be used to monitor the infant’s development and provide early detection of areas in which the infant needs additional supportive services
o	monitoring for signs of increased intracranial pressure (persistent irritability, poor feeding, vomiting) which may indicate that the tubing has become obstructed

Metformin
-	lactic acidosis is a severe complication of metformin, an antidiabetic medication
-	nephropathy caused by administration of iodinated contrast can result in an accumulation of metformin in the bloodstream, which increases the risk for lactic acidosis. Because of this, metformin should be discontinued 24-48 hours before administration of IV iodinated contrast

Breast Engorgement
-	apply ice packs to both breasts for 15-20 minutes every 3-4 hours to reduce blood flow and swelling
-	apply chilled, fresh, cabbage leaves to both breasts 
-	take an anti-inflammatory analgesic to reduce pain
-	maintain firm breast support until milk flow is diminished
-	do not apply heat as it worsens engorgement due to increased blood flow

Diabetic Nephropathy
-	complication of DM caused by microvascular damage to the kidneys
-	injures the glomerulus
-	results in fluid retention and accumulation of waste products 
-	an elevated serum creatinine for a client with DM requires follow up because diabetic nephropathy can lead to end-stage renal disease


Allergic immunotherapy Injections
-	allergy shots
-	triggers an increase in the body’s production of specific immunoglobulins to reduce the client’s allergy symptoms when exposed to specific allergens 
-	small doses of the allergens are injected subcutaneously on a client-specific schedule
-	allergy shots may induce an immediate and potentially fata anaphylactic reaction
-	the client must remain at the facility for 30 minutes after an injection so the nurse can monitor for severe systemic reactions 

Factor Xa inhibitors
-	rivaroxaban (Xarelto), edoxaban, apixaban
-	prevent and treat thromboembolism 
-	avoid taking over the counter medications or supplements that increase bleding risk such as NSAIDs, garlic, ginger 

Inhaled Spacers
-	method for MDI
o	first shake MDI and attach it to the spacer
o	exhale completely to optimize inhalation of the medication
o	place lips tightly around the mouth piece
o	deliver one single puff of medication into spacer
o	take a slow, deep breath and hold it for 10 seconds to allow for effective medication distribution 
o	after the dose, rinse mouth with water to remove any left-over medication from oral mucous membranes
o	spit out the water to ensure no medication is swallowed
o	children under age 12 should use a spacer with the MDI to ensure the entire dose is inhaled appropriately

Linezolid (Zyvox)
-	oxazolidinone 
-	antibiotic prescribed for vancomycin and methillin resistant bacteria, pneumonia, skin infections
-	has MAOI properties, concurrent use with SSRIs increases the risk of serotonin syndrome, a potentially fatal accumulation of serotonin 
-	SSRI can be resumed 24 hours after linezolid therapy has been discontinued

Administering Medication to Infants
-	Place small amounts of the medication at the back of the cheek and allow time for the infant to swallow each amount to decrease risk for choking and ensure all medication is consumed

Tinea Corporis (ringworm)
-	Highly contagious fungal infection on the superficial keratin layers of skin, hair or nails
-	Although implied by the name ringworm, the condition is not caused by a worm infestion, and the nurse should provide further teaching
-	Spread by contact with infection animals, humans, or by touching an infected object 
-	Topical fungal cream is preferred treatment but the infection may require oral antifungal medication 

Vacuum Assisted Vaginal Birth
-	Suction on the fetal head to shorten the pushing stage of labor
-	Excessive traction on the fetal head and neck can cause injury to the brachial plexus nerve roots connecting the neck and arms, most commonly causing erb-duchenne palsy
-	Asymmetric arm movement while testing the moro reflex may indicate fracture or brachial plexus injury
