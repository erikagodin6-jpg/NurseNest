EXAMPLE QUESTIONS

Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client with heart failure who develops sudden weight gain of 2 kg in 24 hours. What is the priority nursing action?

A Encourage increased oral fluids  
B Assess lung sounds and oxygen saturation  
C Administer potassium supplements  
D Restrict physical activity

Correct Answer: B

Correct Answer Explanation:
Sudden weight gain in heart failure often indicates fluid retention. Assessing lung sounds and oxygen saturation helps identify pulmonary edema or worsening heart failure.

Incorrect Options:
A: Increasing fluids would worsen fluid overload.
C: Potassium supplements are not indicated without lab evidence of hypokalemia.
D: While rest may be helpful, assessment of respiratory status is the priority.

Clinical Takeaway:
Rapid weight gain in heart failure usually indicates fluid retention and risk of pulmonary edema.

Exam Pearl:
A weight gain of more than 1–2 kg in 24 hours in heart failure patients suggests fluid overload.

------------------------------------

Question 2

A nurse is assessing a client with suspected renal artery stenosis. Which finding would most strongly support this diagnosis?

A Low blood pressure
B Resistant hypertension
C Bradycardia
D Polyuria

Correct Answer: B

Correct Answer Explanation:
Renal artery stenosis decreases renal perfusion, activating the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system and causing resistant hypertension.

Incorrect Options:
A: Hypotension is not typical of renal artery stenosis.
C: Bradycardia is unrelated to renal artery stenosis.
D: Polyuria is not a characteristic finding.

Clinical Takeaway:
Renal artery stenosis commonly presents with resistant hypertension due to RAAS activation.

Exam Pearl:
A sudden increase in creatinine after starting an ACE inhibitor may indicate bilateral renal artery stenosis.

Question 3

A nurse is caring for a client receiving furosemide. Which electrolyte imbalance is the client most at risk for?

A Hyperkalemia
B Hypokalemia
C Hypercalcemia
D Hypernatremia

Correct Answer: B

Correct Answer Explanation:
Furosemide is a loop diuretic that increases potassium excretion, placing the patient at risk for hypokalemia.

Incorrect Options:
A: Hyperkalemia is more commonly associated with potassium-sparing diuretics.
C: Loop diuretics actually increase calcium excretion.
D: Hypernatremia is not the primary electrolyte imbalance associated with furosemide.

Clinical Takeaway:
Loop diuretics commonly cause hypokalemia due to increased urinary potassium loss.

Exam Pearl:
Monitor potassium levels closely in patients receiving loop diuretics.
