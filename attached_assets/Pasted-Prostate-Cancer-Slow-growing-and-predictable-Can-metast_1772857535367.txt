Prostate Cancer
-	Slow growing and predictable
-	Can metastasize to nearby lymph nodes, liver, lungs, and bone
-	Risks: 
o	Black men, those who have a first-degree relative with prostate cancer, and those above the age of 50
-	Lowering risks:
o	Reduce intake of red meat, animal fat, high-fat dairy products
o	Decrease intake of refined carbohydrates
o	Maintain a healthy weight


HELLP syndrome (hemolysis, elevated liver enzymes, low platelet count)
-	Clinical features: preeclampsia
o	Nausea and vomiting
o	Right upper quadrant abdominal pain
o	Life threatening pregnancy disorder that occurs over 20 weeks gestations 
o	Clients can develop HELPP without hypertension or proteinuria 
o	Clinical manifestations:
	Elevated liver enzymes
	Right upper quadrant pain due to liver swelling
	Malaise
	Nausea
	And decreased platelet count
-	Laboratory findings:
o	Microangiopathic hemolytic anemia
o	Elevated liver enzymes
o	Low platelet count
-	Treatment 
o	Delivery
o	Magnesium for seizure prophylaxis
o	Antihypertensive drugs to prevent stroke
o	Evaluate deep tendon reflexes frequently to assess for hyperreflexia and clonus which may indicate increased central nervous system irritability and precede eclampsia, hyporeflexia may indicate magnesium toxicity 
o	Monitor clotting factors to evaluate bleeding risk and monitor for HELLP syndrome

Thiazide Diuretics (hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone) 
-	Hyponatremia: inhibition of sodium and chloride cotransporters in the distal convoluted tubule – manifests as altered mental status and seizures 
-	Hypokalemia: compensatory rise in renin and aldosterone secretion – manifests as muscle cramps 
-	Hypercalcemia: increased reabsorption in distal tubule – may precipitate or worsen gout attacks 
-	Hyperglycemia and hypercholesterolemia: decreased insulin secretion and increased insulin resistance
-	Hyperuricemia: increased reabsorption in proximal tubule
-	Do not use licorice root with thiazide diuretics as it can augment potassium loss, leading to hypokalemia. Hypokalemia can cause dangerous cardiac arrhythmias. The nurse should discourage the client from taking licorice root and report the client’s use to the health care provider 

Primary Adrenal Insufficiency
-	Etiology: autoimmune adrenalitis
o	Infection
o	Metastatic infiltration
-	Clinical features:
o	Fatigue, weakness, anorexia, weight loss
o	Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
o	Salt craving, postural hypotension
o	Hyperpigmentation
o	Acute adrenal crisis, confusion, hypotension/shock
-	Laboratory findings:
o	Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, eosinophilia
o	Low morning cortisol, high ACTH
-	Treatment: 
o	Glucocorticoids (hydrocortisone, prednisone)
o	Mineralocorticoids (fludrocortisone)
-	Report signs and symptoms of infection immediately. 
-	Contact HCP if increased thirst, hunger, urination that indicate rising blood glucose because the corticosteroids may cause glucose intolerance 
-	Increasing the dose of hydrocortisone as prescribed during times of stress, infection, and before major surgeries 


Infective Endocarditis
-	Requires long term antibiotic therapy 
-	Fever can be persistent for several days even on antibiotics
-	Patients are at risk for systemic embolism and heart failure
-	Decreased capillary oxygen saturation, sob when recumbent, and scattered crackles on lung auscultation indicate fluid backup in the lungs 
-	Borderline low blood pressure and decreased peripheral pulses indicate decreased cardiac output which are concerning for heart failure 

Diabetic ketoacidosis
-	Life threatening
-	Lack of insulin leads to intracellular starvation, body breaks down fat for energy = high levels of acidic ketones (metabolic acidosis) 
-	Pancreatic beta cell destruction prevents insulin release
-	Lack of insulin prevents glucose absorption by tissues
-	Fat and protein are broken down for energy instead
-	Presence of serum ketones 
-	Increased blood glucose levels
-	Kidneys then increase excretion of excess glucose = diuresis = dehydration = hypovolemic shock
-	Sometimes people with type 1 dm have dka as a first time presentation 
-	Risks:
o	Not taking prescribed insulin
o	Physical stress – surgery, illness, trauma
	Sympathetic nervous system is activated 
-	Features
o	Symptoms of hyperglycemia: polyurea, polydipsia
o	Symptoms of acidosis
	Nausea and vomiting
	Fruity odor on client’s breath
	Kussmaul respirations: laboured deep respirations at a constant rate: body is trying to blow off co2 
	Lethargy
o	Features of hypovolemia:
	Dry skin and mucous membranes
	Hypotensive, tachycardic 
	Dizziness and orthostasis
-	Labs
o	Elevated blood glucose above 14
o	Elevated serum and urine ketones
o	Low serum pH (metabolic acidosis)
o	Hyperkalemia (pseudohyperkalemia due to serum depletion of fluids)
-	Nursing:
o	Fluid resuscitation – top priority, need fluid to perfuse extremities
o	Normal saline 
o	Next you can administer IV regular insulin continuously
o	Assess CBG every hour
o	Assess ABG
o	Blood glucose can be normal but you still need to administer insulin to get rid of ketones
o	Once CBG is normal, switch NS to dextrose and keep the insulin running
o	Potassium will help insulin go into the cell so we have to monitor for hypokalemia
	Initiate continuous cardiac monitoring
