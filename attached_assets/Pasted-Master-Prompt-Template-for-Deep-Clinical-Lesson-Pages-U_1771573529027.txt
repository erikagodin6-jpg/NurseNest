Master Prompt Template for Deep Clinical Lesson Pages
Use wording similar to this (you can copy/paste and swap the disease):
PROMPT START
Create an advanced, clinically rigorous nursing lesson page on [DISEASE / CONDITION].
This is not a basic overview. The content must be written at a professional nursing education level suitable for RPN, RN, and NP learners. Assume the reader is preparing for licensing exams and clinical practice.
Use the following mandatory structure and depth requirements:
1. Introduction to the Disease
Provide a conceptual and clinical framing of the condition. Define the disorder, core mechanisms, clinical significance, and typical patient presentations. Avoid superficial definitions.
2. Pathophysiology (Deep Dive)
Provide a mechanistic explanation at the cellular and physiological level.
Include where applicable:
• Cellular injury mechanisms
• Inflammatory cascades
• Neurohormonal pathways
• Hemodynamic changes
• Molecular mediators (cytokines, hormones, receptors, etc.)
• Compensatory mechanisms
• Progression of dysfunction
Explain how and why the disease evolves, not just what happens.
3. Signs and Symptoms WITH Mechanisms
For every major clinical manifestation, explain the underlying physiological cause.
Example expectation:
Not: “Patient develops tachycardia.”
But: “Tachycardia occurs due to [specific compensatory pathway / receptor response / pressure change].”
Link symptoms directly to pathophysiology.
4. Associated Laboratory & Diagnostic Findings
List and explain expected abnormalities.
Include:
• Direction of lab changes
• Why the abnormality occurs
• Clinical interpretation
• Pitfalls / misleading values
Avoid generic lab lists. Tie values specifically to the disease process.
5. Nursing Interventions
Provide detailed, practice-relevant actions.
Include:
• Assessment priorities
• Monitoring parameters
• Rationale for each intervention
• Safety considerations
• Pharmacologic implications (if relevant)
Focus on clinical decision logic, not task lists.
6. Priorities & Emergencies
Describe deterioration patterns, red flags, and immediate actions.
Include:
• Early warning signs
• Life-threatening complications
• Time-sensitive responses
• Escalation logic
Frame this like real clinical judgment.
7. Clinical Pearls & Commonly Missed Points
Include high-value insights learners often misunderstand.
Examples:
• Frequently tested concepts
• Common clinical errors
• Counterintuitive findings
• Exam traps
• Subtle assessment clues
Writing Constraints
• No shallow summaries
• No generic textbook phrasing
• No excessive bullet simplification
• Explanations must emphasize mechanisms and causation
• Write like a high-quality nursing reference, not study notes
Tone: Clear, authoritative, educational, clinically precise.
