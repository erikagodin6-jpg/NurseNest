One more note about the specific HF paragraph you showed
“Cardiac output < 4 L/min” is not consistently used as a bedside exam threshold and may vary by source/context. It’s not dangerous, but it’s not very testable compared to:
orthopnea, crackles, JVD, edema
SpO₂ changes
weight gain thresholds
diuretic monitoring (K+, BP)
when to hold beta blockers/ACEi
red flags: pink frothy sputum, new confusion, hypotension
So you’re better off forcing the model away from “numbers it invents” and toward “exam-usable thresholds” (e.g., “weight gain 2–3 lb/day or 5 lb/week”, “SpO₂ target range per COPD status”, etc.). The decision-density gate helps with that.
