Ventilation vs Oxygenation (Critical Across Ages)
• Ventilation problem → CO₂ issue
• Oxygenation problem → O₂ delivery issue
In children, oxygenation failure occurs much earlier.
✅ Early vs Late Hypoxia (EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD IN PEDS)
Children often show subtle early signs.
Early Pediatric Hypoxia
🚨 Tachycardia (very sensitive sign)
🚨 Tachypnea
🚨 Restlessness / irritability
🚨 Nasal flaring
🚨 Retractions
Late Pediatric Hypoxia
🚨 Bradycardia (VERY ominous)
🚨 Cyanosis
🚨 Lethargy
🚨 Decreased LOC
Critical exam rule:
❌ Bradycardia in a hypoxic child = emergency
✅ Work of Breathing (MORE IMPORTANT THAN SpO₂ ALONE)
Key pediatric distress signs:
• Intercostal retractions
• Subcostal retractions
• Suprasternal retractions
• Grunting
• Head bobbing (infants)
Exam trap:
❌ “Child looks okay but SpO₂ slightly low”
Reality:
Visible retractions = significant distress

🧸 CRITICAL PEDIATRIC RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS FOR REX-PN
These are far more testable than rare congenital disorders.
✅ Bronchiolitis (EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD)
Common cause → Viral (often RSV)
Mechanism:
Inflammation + mucus → Small airway obstruction
Recognition:
• Tachypnea
• Wheezing / crackles
• Retractions
• Feeding difficulty
Priority logic:
🚨 Hydration + WOB + oxygenation
Major exam concept:
❌ Antibiotics NOT routine
✅ Croup (Very Classic Exam Topic)
Mechanism:
Upper airway inflammation → Narrowing
Recognition pattern:
⭐ Barking cough
⭐ Inspiratory stridor
⭐ Hoarseness
Red flags:
🚨 Stridor at rest
🚨 Increasing distress
Exam pearl:
Agitation worsens obstruction
✅ Epiglottitis (Less common but VERY testable)
High-risk emergency recognition question.
Recognition:
🚨 Drooling
🚨 Tripod positioning
🚨 Dysphagia
🚨 Toxic appearance
Critical rule:
❌ Do NOT examine throat aggressively
✅ Pediatric Asthma
Very common exam scenario.
Mechanism:
Bronchoconstriction → Airflow limitation
Recognition:
• Wheeze
• Tachypnea
• Retractions
🚨 Silent chest = severe obstruction
✅ Foreign Body Aspiration (CRITICAL PEDIATRIC TOPIC)
Extremely exam-friendly.
Recognition:
🚨 Sudden onset distress
🚨 Asymmetric breath sounds
🚨 Possible choking history
Priorities:
Airway first — NOT detailed assessment
✅ Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) – Clinical Pattern
Often overlaps with bronchiolitis questions.
Know risks:
• Infants
• Premature babies
• Increased apnea risk
🫁 OXYGEN & PEDIATRIC SAFETY (Major Testing Area)
✅ Children Decompensate Faster
They maintain BP until late → sudden collapse possible.
Exam implication:
Monitor effort, not numbers alone.
✅ Oxygen Use Principles
Oxygen = supportive therapy
Watch for:
• Rising oxygen needs
• Increasing fatigue
• Altered LOC
🫁 DANGEROUS PEDIATRIC RED FLAGS
Students must instantly recognize:
🚨 Stridor at rest
🚨 Grunting
🚨 Severe retractions
🚨 Head bobbing
🚨 Apnea episodes
🚨 Bradycardia
🚨 Decreased LOC
❌ COMMON PEDIATRIC EXAM TRAPS
❌ Waiting for cyanosis
❌ Underestimating tachycardia
❌ Ignoring feeding difficulty
❌ Over-focusing on SpO₂
❌ Missing sudden-onset patterns
❌ Confusing anxiety vs hypoxia
