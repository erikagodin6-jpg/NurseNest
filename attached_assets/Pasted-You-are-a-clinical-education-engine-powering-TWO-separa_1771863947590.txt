You are building NurseNest practice exams with tier-specific blueprints and exam-like scoring.

Platforms:
- Canada site: REX-PN, NCLEX-RN (Canada context), NP (Canada)
- US site: NCLEX-PN, NCLEX-RN, APRN/NP

Goal:
Create practice exams that feel like licensing exams in structure, difficulty curve, and reporting, WITHOUT claiming to replicate proprietary scoring exactly.

Requirements:
A) Exam tiers (separate pools + separate blueprints)
- Tier 1: Practical nurse (RPN/REX-PN in Canada; LPN/NCLEX-PN in US)
- Tier 2: RN (NCLEX-RN)
- Tier 3: NP/APRN (advanced assessment + diagnostics + prescribing within jurisdiction)

B) Exam forms
- Offer multiple exam lengths: 25, 50, 75, 100, 150 questions (configurable).
- Mix question types: single best answer, SATA, prioritization, ordered response, matching/matrix, case/unfolding items.
- Each exam is assembled from blueprint weights by domain + cognitive level.

C) Difficulty model (tiered)
- Each item has:
  - difficulty (1–5)
  - cognitive level (recall, application, analysis, clinical judgment)
  - domain tag(s)
  - safety critical flag
- Tier mapping:
  - Practical nurse: more fundamentals + safe task execution + monitoring + escalation; fewer diagnostic/prescribing items.
  - RN: higher prioritization, delegation, complex med-surg, EBP, systems thinking.
  - NP: differential diagnosis, diagnostics interpretation, prescribing/titration decisions (label “jurisdiction-dependent” when needed).

D) Scoring + “exam-like” results (approximation)
Implement:
- Raw score (% correct)
- Weighted score (weights: difficulty + safety critical items)
- Domain performance breakdown
- Cognitive-level breakdown
- Pass probability indicator (based on calibrated thresholds you define)
- Confidence band (e.g., low/medium/high confidence) based on number of items + consistency
- Review mode: explanations + remediations
- Timed mode: pacing, time per question, missed question review

Do NOT claim “this is exactly NCLEX scoring.”
Use language: “Exam-style scoring approximation.”
