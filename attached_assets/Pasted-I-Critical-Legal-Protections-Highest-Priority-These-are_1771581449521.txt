I. Critical Legal Protections (Highest Priority)
These are non-negotiable for a healthcare-adjacent platform.
1. Terms of Service (Absolutely Essential)
This is your primary legal shield.
It must explicitly define:
• Educational purpose only
• No medical / clinical advice
• No guarantees of outcomes
• Payment & refund rules
• Subscription billing mechanics
• Intellectual property ownership
• Account termination rights
• Limitation of liability
• Governing law (important for Canada)
Without this, disputes become dangerous.
2. Educational / Medical Disclaimer (Separate Page + Footer)
You already hinted at this in FAQ, but it must exist as a formal standalone disclaimer.
Key statements:
• Content is for educational purposes only
• Not medical advice
• Not a substitute for licensed clinical judgment
• Users must follow institutional policies
• No provider–patient relationship created
Place links:
✔ Footer
✔ Signup / payment screen
✔ Possibly lesson pages
3. Privacy Policy (Legally Required in Many Regions)
Especially important since you use Stripe.
Must state:
• What data is collected
• How it is used
• Third-party processors (Stripe, analytics, etc.)
• Cookies / tracking
• Data retention principles
Even if minimal data is stored, you still need this.
4. Refund & Billing Policy (Chargeback Prevention Tool)
Never rely on vague language.
Define:
• Digital product nature
• When refunds apply / do not apply
• Cancellation mechanics
• Billing cycles
• Trial rules
Chargebacks destroy subscription businesses.
Clear rules reduce them dramatically.
II. Protections Specific to Nursing / Clinical Content
Very important for NurseNest.
5. “No Clinical Advice” Boundary Reinforcement
Include wording across site:
• NurseNest does not provide medical advice
• Clinical decisions must rely on licensed providers & policies
• Scenarios are educational simulations
Avoid language implying:
❌ “Do this in practice”
❌ “Correct treatment is…”
Safer phrasing:
✔ “Educational considerations”
✔ “Typical clinical responses may include…”
6. No Exam Body Affiliation Statement (Extremely Important)
Protects you from legal headaches.
Explicitly state:
• Not affiliated with NCLEX / NCSBN / regulatory bodies
• Questions are original
• Platform is independent
Many education startups get into trouble here.
7. Scope of Practice Sensitivity
Since you address RPN / RN / NP:
Avoid definitive legal claims about scope.
Safer phrasing:
✔ “Role considerations may vary by jurisdiction”
✔ “Always follow regulatory guidance”
III. User Safety & Ethical Protections
Rarely discussed but important.
8. Clinical Responsibility Reminder
Especially near simulations / case studies:
• Educational tool only
• Real patient care requires professional judgment
• Follow employer & regulatory standards
Protects both you and learners.
9. Psychological Expectation Management
Prevent unrealistic beliefs:
• No guarantees of exam success
• Platform supports learning, not certification
Reduces refund disputes and complaints.
IV. Intellectual Property Protection (Very Important)
Frequently ignored by creators.
10. Content Usage Restrictions
Inside Terms:
Prohibit:
• Downloading / scraping
• Redistribution
• Sharing accounts
• Commercial reuse
Without this, piracy becomes rampant.
11. Copyright Notice
Footer example:
© NurseNest. All rights reserved.
Signals ownership and professionalism.
V. Financial Risk Protections (Critical for Subscriptions)
12. Subscription Clarity (Legally Safer Billing)
On pricing page & checkout:
Clearly state:
• Recurring billing
• Billing interval
• Cancellation rules
Ambiguity → chargebacks.
13. Avoid Dangerous Claims
Do NOT say:
❌ “Pass your exam guaranteed”
❌ “Clinically proven”
Use:
✔ “Designed to support…”
✔ “Built to strengthen…”
VI. Trust & Credibility Protections (Indirect but Powerful)
Higher trust = fewer disputes.
14. Professional Positioning
Avoid gimmicky language or visuals implying entertainment.
Healthcare education benefits from:
✔ Calm authority
✔ Precision
✔ Clinical tone
Trust reduces legal and financial friction.
Minimal Protection Stack for NurseNest (If Prioritizing)
If you implement nothing else immediately, secure:
Terms of Service
Privacy Policy
Educational / Medical Disclaimer
Refund & Billing Policy
No Exam Affiliation Statement
Copyright Notice
This covers most real-world risks.
Important Perspective
Protection is not paranoia — it is infrastructure.
Well-written policies:
✔ Reduce disputes
✔ Reduce refunds
✔ Reduce chargebacks
✔ Increase perceived legitimacy
✔ Increase conversions
Ironically, safer platforms often sell better.
