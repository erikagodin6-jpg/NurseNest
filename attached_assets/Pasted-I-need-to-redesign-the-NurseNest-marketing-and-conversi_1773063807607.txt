I need to redesign the NurseNest marketing and conversion system so the homepage, landing pages, pricing pages, upgrade prompts, and checkout flow are role-aware and track-specific.

Current problem:
The platform is becoming more tier-native inside the product, but the marketing and conversion experience still feels too generic. I do not want RPN/LVN, RN, and NP users to land on the same generalized messaging and then have to figure out whether NurseNest is meant for them. I want each audience to immediately feel seen, understood, and guided toward the exact product that matches their exam path.

GOAL:
Build a role-aware marketing and conversion architecture for:
- Free / undecided visitors
- RPN/LVN visitors
- RN visitors
- NP visitors
- Returning logged-in users upgrading from free to paid

The experience should feel premium, clear, personalized, conversion-focused, and emotionally aligned with the learner’s stage, goals, and exam pressure.

==================================================
PART 1 — CORE CONVERSION PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Implement a role-aware marketing system where marketing pages and upgrade flows dynamically adapt based on:
- selected track
- detected user role
- current subscription
- last viewed tier
- onboarding choice
- URL route
- query param
- saved preference

The system must support:
- generic entry for first-time visitors
- guided selection into RPN / RN / NP paths
- persistent track memory after selection
- track-specific landing pages

- track-specific checkout summaries
- role-matched testimonials and value propositions

The marketing experience must not feel one-size-fits-all.
It should feel like NurseNest deeply understands each learner’s exam journey.

==================================================
PART 2 — AUDIENCE SEGMENTS
==================================================

Create separate messaging systems for these audiences:

1. UNDECIDED / GENERAL VISITOR
- not logged in
- has not selected a tier yet
- may not know the difference between tracks
- needs guidance into the right pathway

2. RPN/LVN VISITOR
- practical nursing learner
- preparing for REx-PN, CPNRE-style practical nursing review, or equivalent PN/LVN prep path depending on the site configuration
- wants foundational, exam-relevant, confidence-building prep

3. RN VISITOR
- preparing for RN-level licensure exam / readiness
- wants deeper clinical judgment, prioritization, management of care, and stronger exam simulation

4. NP VISITOR
- advanced practice learner
- wants graduate-level board prep, advanced assessment, diagnostic reasoning, prescribing, and management planning

5. FREE LOGGED-IN USER
- has already entered the product
- may have used free quizzes or sample lessons
- needs a stronger upgrade flow tailored to their selected track and usage behavior

PART 22 — UX QUALITY RULES
==================================================

Follow these strict rules:

1. No generic conversion copy when track intent is known
If the system knows the learner wants RPN, RN, or NP, show track-specific value immediately.

2. No cross-track confusion
Do not use RN language on RPN pages.
Do not use beginner wording on NP pages.

3. The user should feel understood within 3 seconds
The headline, subheadline, and CTA should instantly signal “this is for me.”

4. Free users must see a clear path forward
Guide them to the correct track and then to a clear upgrade action.

5. Paid users should feel like they bought a precise product
Checkout and post-purchase messaging must reinforce exactly what they unlocked.
