Create a fully developed Pre-Nursing Foundations Program designed to prepare students BEFORE entering nursing school or health sciences programs.

Academic Level:
Content must reflect university/college prerequisite rigor, not simplified summaries. Lessons must build deep conceptual understanding and eliminate common knowledge gaps that cause early nursing school difficulty.

Critical Scope Rules (must follow strictly):
• DO NOT include pathophysiology
• DO NOT include disease mechanisms or clinical management
• DO NOT include drug classes or therapeutics
• Foundational pharmacology principles are allowed ONLY at theory level (no drug lists)

Program Goal:
Produce a structured curriculum that systematically builds foundational knowledge required for success in anatomy, physiology, dosage calculations, microbiology, research, statistics, and early nursing theory.

Organize content into comprehensive modules covering ALL of the following domains:

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 1: Cellular & Molecular Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Cell structure and organelles
• Plasma membrane dynamics
• Diffusion, facilitated diffusion, active transport
• Osmosis and tonicity
• Ion gradients and electrochemical principles
• Cell signaling and receptors
• Protein synthesis basics
• DNA, RNA, gene expression foundations
• Cellular metabolism and ATP production
• Structure–function relationships

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 2: Basic Chemistry for Health Sciences
------------------------------------------------------------
• Atomic structure
• Chemical bonds
• Molecules vs compounds
• Ions and electrolytes (chemistry focus)
• Acids, bases, pH
• Buffers (conceptual)
• Solutions and concentrations
• Dilutions and concentration reasoning
• Chemical reactions relevant to biology

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 3: Human Anatomy Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Anatomical terminology
• Body planes and directional terms
• Body cavities and membranes
• Structural organization of the body
• Tissue types and histology basics
• Organ systems overview
• Structure–function principles

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 4: Human Physiology Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Homeostasis and set points
• Negative vs positive feedback
• Gradients, pressures, flows
• Membrane potentials (normal physiology)
• Electrical signaling fundamentals (non-pathologic)
• Transport dynamics across membranes
• Integration across organ systems (big-picture regulation)

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 5: Medical Terminology Mastery
------------------------------------------------------------
• Word roots, prefixes, suffixes
• Decoding medical language logically (not memorization lists)
• High-frequency terms used in A&P and healthcare
• Abbreviation interpretation principles and common pitfalls

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 6: Microbiology Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Types of microorganisms and basic structure
• Microbial growth and reproduction concepts
• Normal flora vs contamination concepts
• Transmission basics (conceptual)
• Sterility vs disinfection vs antisepsis (definitions + reasoning)
• Infection prevention foundations (non-clinical depth)

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 7: Fluids & Electrolytes Foundations (Normal Physiology Focus)
------------------------------------------------------------
• Body fluid compartments
• Osmotic and oncotic concepts (conceptual)
• Electrolyte roles in normal function (no disorders)
• Fluid shifts and tonicity reasoning
• Acid–base foundations (pH, buffers) at a conceptual level (no disease states)

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 8: Foundations of Pharmacology (Theory Only)
------------------------------------------------------------
• Pharmacokinetics (ADME)
• Pharmacodynamics and receptors
• Dose–response relationships
• Therapeutic window and safety margin
• Routes of administration principles
• Medication safety foundations (rights of medication administration, error prevention)
Do not include drug classes or therapeutics.

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 9: Dosage Calculations & Math Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Fractions, decimals, ratios, proportions
• Dimensional analysis method
• Unit conversions (metric and common healthcare units)
• Oral dose calculations
• Liquid medication calculations
• Basic IV rate logic (mL/hr; gtt/min if relevant)
Constraints:
• Use pharmaceutically realistic “on hand” values and concentrations
• Avoid random/nonexistent drug strengths
• Provide clear step-by-step solutions and common error traps

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 10: Research & Evidence-Based Practice Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• What EBP is and why it matters
• Types of clinical/research questions (PICO basics)
• Study designs overview (conceptual)
• Levels of evidence and evidence hierarchies
• Validity, reliability, bias (intro)
• How to read a paper structurally (abstract to discussion)
• Critical appraisal mindset (intro; no advanced biostat yet)

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 11: Statistics for Nursing & Critical Appraisal (Dedicated Module)
------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: Provide the statistics literacy nurses need to interpret research, appraise evidence, and understand clinical claims.

Include:
• Data types: nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio
• Distributions: normal vs skewed; mean/median/mode
• Measures of spread: range, variance, standard deviation, IQR
• Sampling concepts: population vs sample; sampling bias; power (conceptual)
• Confidence intervals: interpretation and clinical meaning
• P-values: what they do and do not mean; common misinterpretations
• Hypothesis testing basics: null vs alternative (conceptual)
• Type I vs Type II error
• Correlation vs causation
• Effect sizes (conceptual) and why they matter
• Common statistical tests at a recognition/interpretation level:
  - t-test, chi-square, ANOVA (what question they answer)
  - regression (basic idea)
• Risk measures used in health research:
  - absolute risk, relative risk, odds ratio
  - absolute risk reduction, relative risk reduction
  - number needed to treat (NNT) / number needed to harm (NNH)
• Sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV (interpretation-focused)
• Graph literacy:
  - reading bar graphs, histograms, box plots, scatter plots, forest plots
• Practical critical appraisal:
  - interpreting “statistically significant” vs “clinically significant”
  - spotting misleading graphs and reporting

Rules:
• Keep explanations intuitive but accurate (no heavy math derivations)
• Use healthcare-relevant examples without drifting into disease pathophysiology
• Teach interpretation and reasoning over calculation

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 12: Academic Survival & Study Strategy
------------------------------------------------------------
• Cognitive load management
• Active recall vs passive study
• Spaced repetition principles
• Concept mapping and frameworks
• Time management and study planning
• Common failure patterns in nursing school and how to avoid them
• Exam reasoning strategies (pre-nursing level)

------------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 13: Communication & Professional Foundations
------------------------------------------------------------
• Professional communication basics
• Therapeutic communication foundations (conceptual)
• SBAR logic (conceptual)
• Documentation principles (foundations)
• Team communication basics and professionalism
• Error prevention mindset and situational awareness

Lesson Construction Rules (apply to EVERY module):
• Provide deep explanations with cause–effect reasoning (non-pathologic)
• Clarify commonly misunderstood concepts and typical student traps
• Avoid shallow 1–2 sentence content and avoid filler
• Prefer full sentences and structured teaching sections over bullet-only summaries
• Maintain conceptual continuity across modules so later modules build logically on earlier ones

Quiz Requirement (Mandatory):
At the end of EACH module, generate a quiz containing 10–20 questions.
Quiz rules:
• Focus on conceptual understanding and applied reasoning (not trivial recall)
• Include a mix of MCQ and SATA-style questions if appropriate
• Provide a full answer key with rationales
• Avoid ambiguous wording
• For calculations: use realistic medication formats and provide step-by-step solutions

Tone & Quality:
Professional, academic, structured, instructional.
Write like premium pre-health sciences learning material.

Output Expectations:
• Fully structured curriculum across all modules above
• Comprehensive lesson development with strong depth
• Quiz (10–20 Q) + answer rationales at the end of each module
• Strictly avoid pathophysiology, disease management, and pharmacology beyond foundations
