1. Management of Care / Clinical Judgment
These are extremely high-value because exams are heavily weighted toward decision-making.
Include items on:
✔ Prioritization frameworks (ABCs, safety vs non-safety)
✔ Delegation & assignment logic
✔ Scope of practice distinctions
✔ Ethical dilemmas
✔ Informed consent
✔ Documentation traps
✔ Unexpected findings
These questions are more important than disease trivia.
2. Safety & Risk Reduction (Major Exam Driver)
High-frequency test domain.
Topics:
✔ Infection prevention & control
✔ Isolation precautions
✔ Medication safety
✔ High-alert drugs
✔ Falls / restraints
✔ Post-op complications
✔ Adverse reactions
✔ Sentinel events
Students struggle here → strong subscription driver.
3. Pharmacology (Revenue Goldmine)
Must be very deep.
Organize by:
Drug Families
✔ Cardiovascular
✔ Endocrine
✔ CNS
✔ Respiratory
✔ Anti-infectives
✔ GI / Renal
✔ Emergency drugs
Test:
✔ Mechanisms of action
✔ Side effect patterns
✔ Contraindications
✔ Drug interactions
✔ Nursing implications
✔ Monitoring priorities
Avoid simple memorization.

🧠 Cognitive Skills Layer (Exam Realism Engine)
Across ALL topics, deliberately create items that test:
✔ Prioritization
✔ Early vs late signs
✔ Expected vs unexpected findings
✔ Most concerning vs benign
✔ Safety traps
✔ Delegation errors
✔ Mechanism-based reasoning
This is what makes a Qbank feel “like the exam.”
📊 How Many Diseases Should NurseNest Cover?
There is no fixed number, but competitive perception typically requires:
✔ All common high-frequency conditions
✔ All major system categories
✔ All safety-critical scenarios
✔ Repeated exposure to core diseases
Realistically:
Core diseases repeatedly tested → ~150–250 high-yield conditions drive most exam items
Questions multiply around complications, meds, priorities
A 3,000+ question bank feels psychologically competitive regardless of disease count.
🚀 Strategic Insight for NurseNest
Your advantage is NOT:
❌ “More diseases”
Your advantage is:
✔ Better rationales
✔ Better cognitive training
✔ Better emotional experience
✔ Better clarity
✔ Better safety logic
