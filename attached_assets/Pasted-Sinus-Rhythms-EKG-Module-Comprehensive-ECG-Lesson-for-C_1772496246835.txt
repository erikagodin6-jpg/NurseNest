Lesson 1: The Five-Step Rhythm Analysis Method
Accurate ECG interpretation requires a structured approach. The five steps must be applied to every rhythm strip in the same order.
Step 1: Rhythm Regularity






4
Measure from the peak of one R wave to the peak of the next across the entire strip.
A rhythm is considered regular if the R–R intervals are equal or vary by ≤ 1.5 small boxes.
If the variation exceeds 2 small boxes → rhythm is irregular.
This step determines ventricular regularity.
Step 2: Heart Rate Determination
For Regular Rhythms – The 1500 Method
Count the number of small boxes between two consecutive R waves.
Heart Rate = 1500 ÷ number of small boxes
Example:
1500 ÷ 25 = 60 bpm
This method is the most precise for regular rhythms.
For Irregular Rhythms – The Six-Second Method
Count the number of QRS complexes in 6 seconds and multiply by 10.
Example:
8 beats in 6 seconds → 80 bpm
Step 3: P Wave Morphology






4
Lead II is the standard reference lead in rhythm monitoring.
Normal sinus P waves are:
Upright
Rounded
Uniform in shape
Precede each QRS
Abnormal findings may include:
Inverted P waves
Peaked P waves
Flattened or notched waves
Absent or buried P waves
Changes in morphology may indicate altered atrial conduction or pacemaker shift.
Step 4: PR Interval (PRi)
Measure from the beginning of the P wave to the beginning of the QRS complex.
Normal PR interval:
0.12 – 0.20 seconds (3–5 small boxes)
It must:
Be measured on every beat
Remain consistent
A variable PR interval must be reported and described.
Step 5: QRS Complex






4
QRS represents ventricular depolarization.
Normal duration:
0.06 – 0.10 seconds
Assess:
Width
Morphology
Consistency
Changes may indicate conduction delays or ventricular origin.
Lesson 2: Sinus Rhythm Overview
Sinus rhythms originate from the sinoatrial (SA) node, located in the right atrium.






4
Sinus rhythms share these features:
Normal appearing P waves
PR interval within normal limits
Narrow QRS complex
Origin at SA node
Differences between sinus rhythms involve:
Rate
Regularity
Presence or absence of pauses
Lesson 3: Normal Sinus Rhythm (NSR)
Diagnostic Criteria
Rate: 60–100 bpm
Regular rhythm
Upright uniform P waves in lead II
1:1 P to QRS ratio
PR interval 0.12–0.20 seconds
QRS 0.06–0.10 seconds






4
Normal sinus rhythm is the only rhythm in which all five steps are normal.
Clinical significance:
Indicates intact SA node function and normal conduction pathway.
