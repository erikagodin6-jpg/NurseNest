NurseNest Study Guide Editing Checklist
(Your Quality Control System Before Publishing)
Use this every time before clicking “Store-Ready.”
A. Clinical Accuracy Pass (Non-Negotiable)
Confirm lab values (separate CA vs US ranges clearly)
Confirm medication mechanisms (no vague statements)
Confirm scope differences (RPN vs RN vs NP)
Remove outdated guidelines
Ensure contraindications are correct
If any of these are wrong → product credibility collapses.
B. Exam Relevance Pass
Delete:
Long textbook-style definitions
Rare complications unlikely to be tested
Patho deep dives that don’t change nursing care
Add:
“Most Tested Concept”
“Classic Exam Trap”
“If you see this, think this”
Prioritization cues
Example:
Instead of:
“Hyperkalemia can affect cardiac conduction.”
Write:
“On exams: hyperkalemia → peaked T waves FIRST.”
C. Prioritization Upgrade Pass
Add:
ABC prioritization statements
What is the FIRST action?
What is most concerning?
What requires immediate provider notification?
Students pay for clinical reasoning — not summaries.
D. Differentiation Upgrade
Add comparison tables:
DKA vs HHS
Left vs Right Heart Failure
COPD vs Asthma
Hypo vs Hyperthyroidism
Comparison tables increase perceived value dramatically.
E. Monetization Layer
Before publishing, ask:
Would a student screenshot this?
If yes → good.
If no → add:
Clean visual table
Flowchart
Step-by-step algorithm
Quick-reference summary page
2️⃣ Standardized AI Draft Template (By Tier)

🔹 REx-PN / NCLEX-PN Version
Create a structured exam-focused study guide for [TOPIC].

Audience:
- REx-PN (Canada)
- NCLEX-PN (US)

Requirements:
- Clear separation between Canadian and US terminology where relevant
- Prioritize high-yield exam content
- Avoid textbook filler
- Focus on nursing actions and prioritization

Include:
1. Learning objectives
2. Pathophysiology (concise, clinically relevant)
3. Classic signs and symptoms
4. Assessment findings
5. Labs (include Canadian and US normal ranges if different)
6. First-line medications with mechanism
7. Nursing interventions (prioritized)
8. Complications
9. Patient teaching
10. 20 exam-style multiple choice questions
11. Detailed rationales explaining why each distractor is incorrect

Tone: Direct, exam-focused, high-yield.

🔹 NP Version

Differential diagnosis
Referral criteria
