Generate mock REX-PN style nursing exam questions that replicate licensure-level clinical reasoning and decision-making. Questions must test application, prioritization, safety, and nursing judgment rather than factual recall.
Question Formats Required
Create questions using a balanced mix of the following item types:
• Multiple-choice (single best answer)
• Multiple-response (select all that apply)
• Ordered response (sequencing / prioritization)
• Fill-in-the-blank (calculation or numeric response)
• Hot spot (visual identification — describe image context)
Distribute formats realistically, similar to competency-based nursing exams.
Clinical Framing Rules
All questions must:
• Be scenario-based and patient-centered
• Reflect realistic nursing practice situations
• Require clinical judgment or decision-making
• Avoid trivial memorization or definitions
• Prioritize safety and risk recognition
Include common licensure-style wording patterns such as:
• “What is the nurse’s priority action?”
• “Which finding requires immediate follow-up?”
• “Which interventions are appropriate?”
• “Place the actions in the correct order.”
Cognitive Level Constraints
Questions must assess:
✔ Clinical reasoning
✔ Priority setting (ABCs, safety, deterioration)
✔ Interpretation of patient data
✔ Pathophysiology application
✔ Medication safety when relevant
Avoid:
❌ Simple recall questions
❌ Purely definitional stems
❌ Academic trivia
Distractor Engineering Rules (Critical)
Incorrect answers must be plausible but unsafe, incomplete, or lower priority. Avoid obviously wrong distractors.
Use realistic nursing errors such as:
• Incorrect prioritization
• Unsafe timing
• Scope-inappropriate actions
• Misinterpretation of findings
• Partially correct but non-priority interventions
Answer & Rationale Requirements
For EVERY question provide:
• Correct answer(s) clearly indicated
• Detailed rationale explaining WHY correct
• Explanation of WHY incorrect options are wrong
• Focus on clinical logic, not restating facts
Rationales must teach decision-making and exam thinking patterns.
Item-Specific Instructions
Multiple-Choice:
Four options only. Single best answer. No ambiguity.
Multiple-Response (SATA):
Include 5–7 options. More than one correct. Avoid “all or none obvious” patterns.
Ordered Response:
Require sequencing of clinical actions based on safety and workflow logic.
Fill-in-the-Blank:
Use medication calculations, IV rates, or safe numeric decisions. Provide correct value.
Hot Spot:
Describe the clinical image context (e.g., ECG strip, injection site, assessment finding) and identify correct target conceptually.
Tone & Exam Fidelity
Questions must resemble Canadian practical nurse licensure cognition:
• Safety-focused
• Priority-driven
• Clinically realistic
• No trick wording or riddles
• No excessive academic language
