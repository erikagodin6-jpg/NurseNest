Expand the Anatomy & Physiology modules into a fully developed, university-level course that is academically rigorous, conceptually deep, and structured like a serious pre-health sciences program.

Depth & Academic Standard:

Content must reflect university-level expectations, not simplified nursing summaries. Explanations should develop true understanding of biological mechanisms, not memorization lists.

Coverage Requirements:

Develop a complete head-to-toe Anatomy & Physiology curriculum including:

• Cellular and molecular foundations of human biology
• Tissue types and structural organization
• Integumentary system
• Skeletal system
• Muscular system
• Nervous system
• Endocrine system
• Cardiovascular system
• Respiratory system
• Renal/urinary system
• Gastrointestinal system
• Immune/lymphatic system
• Reproductive system
• Homeostasis and regulatory integration

Cell Biology & Foundational Mechanisms (Critical):

Include deep explanations of:

• Cell structure and organelles
• Membrane dynamics and transport mechanisms
• Ion gradients and electrochemical principles
• Cellular communication and signaling
• Protein synthesis and genetic control
• Cellular metabolism and energy production
• Cell cycle and replication basics
• Structure–function relationships

Physiology Standards:

Explain HOW systems function mechanistically:

• Cause–effect relationships
• Feedback loops
• Gradients, pressures, flows
• Electrical signaling principles
• Mechanical forces where relevant
• Chemical signaling logic
• Functional integration across systems

Avoid shallow descriptions such as “the heart pumps blood.” Instead explain:

• Pressure generation
• Electrical conduction logic
• Valve mechanics
• Vascular dynamics
• Functional consequences

Lesson Construction Rules:

For EVERY system/module:

• Provide structured sections
• Explain mechanisms and reasoning
• Clarify commonly misunderstood concepts
• Use precise biological terminology
• Emphasize structure–function relationships
• Avoid oversimplified textbook language
• Avoid pathophysiology and disease discussions unless essential for explaining normal physiology boundaries

Tone & Style:

Professional, academic, clear, intelligent.
Write like a high-quality university learning resource.

Quiz Requirements (Mandatory):

At the end of EACH module, generate a quiz containing 10–20 questions.

Quiz design rules:

• Questions must test understanding, not trivial recall
• Use applied reasoning and conceptual testing
• Include clinical-style logic where appropriate but without pathophysiology
• Include answer key with explanations
• Avoid ambiguous or poorly constructed questions
• Avoid unrealistic numbers or nonsense scenarios

Example question style:

• Mechanism-based reasoning
• Functional prediction
• Structure–function relationships
• Cause–effect logic

Quality Control:

• No shallow filler content
• No bullet-only summaries
• No generic high-school explanations
• Content must feel cohesive and instructional
• Modules should logically build on each other
• Ensure internal conceptual consistency

Output Expectations:

• Fully expanded modules
• Clear hierarchical organization
• Deep conceptual explanations
• Quiz at end of each module
• Answer rationales included
