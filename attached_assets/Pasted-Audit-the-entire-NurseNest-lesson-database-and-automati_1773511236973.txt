Audit the entire NurseNest lesson database and automatically fill every empty, placeholder, incomplete, or underdeveloped lesson across all tiers as fast as possible without lowering quality.

GOAL
Find all lessons across every tier that are missing meaningful educational content and fully populate them with high-quality, exam-aligned, clinically accurate lesson material that is ready for production.

TIERS TO INCLUDE
- RPN
- RN
- NP
- Allied Health
- New Grad
- Imaging
- Any other published or partially built educational tier/subdomain in the project

WHAT COUNTS AS AN “EMPTY” OR “INCOMPLETE” LESSON
A lesson should be flagged for repair if ANY of the following are true:
- title exists but body/content is empty
- lesson only has 1 to 2 short paragraphs
- placeholder text exists
- “coming soon” type content exists
- headings exist without educational body text
- lesson is missing key clinical teaching sections
- lesson has weak low-detail AI filler content
- lesson lacks exam relevance
- lesson lacks nursing interventions or clinical application where appropriate
- lesson has no image when one should exist
- lesson has broken formatting or blank rendered sections
- lesson content is too short to be useful for a student

DO NOT
- do not overwrite strong existing lessons that are already comprehensive
- do not duplicate content across multiple lessons
- do not insert generic filler
- do not add cartoonish or childish visuals
- do not label lesson titles with “RPN”, “RN”, or “NP” if the user is already inside that tier
- do not break slugs, routing, SEO, existing analytics, progress tracking, or admin controls
- do not publish junk just to fill space

FOR EACH INCOMPLETE LESSON, GENERATE A FULL LESSON USING THIS STRUCTURE
1. Clean concise lesson title
2. Brief overview/introduction
3. Definition or condition overview
4. Relevant anatomy and physiology or underlying foundations
5. Causes / pathophysiology / mechanism
6. Risk factors
7. Signs and symptoms / clinical presentation
8. Assessment findings
9. Diagnostics / labs / imaging / relevant investigations
10. Management / treatments / interventions
11. Nursing considerations
12. Medications if relevant
13. Complications
14. Patient teaching
15. Clinical pearls
16. Exam tips / what students are likely to be tested on
17. Summary / key takeaways

ADAPT DEPTH BY TIER
RPN lessons:
- practical, foundational, clinically clear
- strong focus on common conditions, safe care, straightforward patho, assessment, interventions, patient teaching
- suitable for REx-PN style learning

RN lessons:
- more depth in pathophysiology, prioritization, complex assessment, nursing judgment, delegation awareness, complications
- suitable for NCLEX-RN level

NP lessons:
- advanced pathophysiology, differential diagnosis framing, advanced assessment, diagnostics, prescribing/treatment considerations where appropriate, red flags, escalation, clinical decision-making
- suitable for advanced practice learners

Allied Health lessons:
- tailor language and content to the profession or specialty area represented
- still maintain high exam and practice relevance

New Grad lessons:
- focus on transition to practice, prioritization, communication, documentation, workflow, professionalism, common real-world scenarios

Imaging lessons:
- focus on modality purpose, anatomy, indications, interpretation basics, contrast, prep, contraindications, patient safety

CONTENT QUALITY RULES
- content must be clinically accurate, useful, and educationally rich
- no vague filler language
- no repetitive phrasing
- no obvious AI fluff
- use scannable formatting with strong headings and short paragraphs
- include bullet lists only where actually helpful
- explain concepts clearly but at the correct level for the tier
- ensure all lessons feel premium and production-ready
- prioritize topics that are highly testable and clinically relevant
- maintain consistent NurseNest tone: clean, modern, professional, supportive, high-value

IMAGE RULES
For lessons that should include a visual:
- add an appropriate realistic, polished educational image
- use medically credible visual style
- no flat cartoon style
- no exaggerated or childish icons
- images should match the lesson topic and feel premium
- if an existing site image already fits the lesson, reuse it
- if no suitable image exists, generate or assign a new one through the existing image pipeline
- ensure header images render correctly and do not omit the lesson topic visually
- rectangular lesson-header format when appropriate

TITLE CLEANUP
- remove redundant tier labels from lesson titles
- shorten overly long lesson titles while preserving SEO and clarity
- ensure titles are readable on mobile and desktop
- keep titles clinically specific

ADMIN / SYSTEM TASKS
1. Scan the database and identify every lesson with missing or weak content
2. Produce a repair queue grouped by tier
3. Fill lessons in batches
4. Save directly to the production-ready lesson records used by the published site
5. Preserve IDs, slugs, and routing unless a slug is clearly broken
6. Mark repaired lessons as complete/reviewed if your workflow supports that
7. Log what was updated
8. Provide a final summary by tier:
   - total lessons scanned
   - total repaired
   - total skipped because already high quality
   - total still requiring manual review

PRIORITIZATION ORDER
1. Published lessons visible to users that are blank or nearly blank
2. Core high-traffic nursing topics
3. RN and RPN foundational systems/topics
4. NP advanced clinical topics
5. Allied health and imaging gaps
6. New grad professional practice gaps

REPAIR THRESHOLDS
Automatically repair any lesson that meets one or more of these:
- under a minimum useful word count
- missing 4 or more core sections
- no meaningful exam relevance
- no nursing/clinical application
- blank rendered body
- only placeholder text
- weak low-detail content compared with surrounding lessons

FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS
- clean heading hierarchy
- mobile-friendly spacing
- no broken HTML/markdown rendering
- no duplicated sections
- no random capitalization
- no unnecessary divider lines
- no emojis

EXAM ALIGNMENT
Where relevant, align lesson framing to:
- REx-PN style readiness for RPN
- NCLEX-RN style readiness for RN
- advanced practice diagnostic reasoning for NP
- role-specific exam or practice readiness for allied categories

SAFETY / ACCURACY
- do not invent dangerous clinical guidance
- where evidence-sensitive guidance is needed, stay within standard educational nursing scope
- include escalation/red flag content where clinically appropriate
- avoid unsupported absolutes

OUTPUT REQUIRED
After the job finishes, return:
1. a full summary of all tiers processed
2. which lessons were filled
3. which lessons were skipped and why
4. any lessons still too ambiguous for safe auto-generation
5. any image failures or formatting issues encountered
6. recommendations for the next batch of lesson improvements

IMPORTANT
This is a production content quality task, not a demo task.
Prioritize speed, but not at the expense of clinical usefulness or professionalism.
Every repaired lesson should feel like a premium NurseNest lesson that a paying student would consider valuable.
