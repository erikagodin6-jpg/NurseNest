Create structured lessons on rare, high-risk, and “don’t miss” diseases for NurseNest.

These lessons must be tiered by licensing level and scope:

- RPN (Canada)
- REx-PN (Canada exam)
- RN (Canada)
- NCLEX-RN (USA)
- NP (Canada + USA)
- AANP / ANCC
- CNPLE (future Canadian NP exam)

IMPORTANT:
Each disease must be presented at the appropriate scope and depth for each tier. Do NOT teach NP-level management to RPN students. Do NOT under-teach pathophysiology to NP students.

=====================================
STRUCTURE FOR EACH DISEASE LESSON
=====================================

For each condition include:

1. Overview
   - What it is
   - Why it is dangerous
   - Why it appears on licensing exams

2. Pathophysiology
   - Tiered by level:
     - RPN: simplified mechanism
     - RN: system-level mechanism
     - NP: cellular, hormonal, or molecular detail when appropriate

3. Clinical Presentation
   - Classic board-style presentation
   - Atypical presentation
   - Red flag symptoms

4. Diagnostic Criteria
   - Required labs
   - Imaging
   - Diagnostic thresholds
   - What confirms diagnosis
   - What rules it out

5. Differential Diagnosis
   - Most commonly confused conditions
   - How to distinguish

6. Management (Tiered)
   - RPN: recognition + escalation + monitoring
   - RN: assessment + interventions + medication awareness
   - NP: prescribing, referral logic, risk stratification, monitoring

7. When to Refer / When to Send to ED
   - Clear urgency categories

8. Licensing Exam Focus
   - REx-PN trap concepts
   - NCLEX-style question traps
   - AANP/ANCC advanced reasoning points
   - CNPLE cross-system complexity

9. LOFT-Style Question Set
   - 5–10 per tier
   - Mix of:
     - Single best answer
     - SATA
     - Prioritization
     - Red flag recognition
     - Prescribing logic (NP only)

=====================================
TIER DEPTH GUIDELINES
=====================================

RPN:
- Focus on recognition
- Vital signs instability
- Red flags
- Escalation and reporting

REx-PN:
- Prioritization
- Safety
- Monitoring
- Early complication recognition

RN:
- Assessment findings
- Interventions
- Lab interpretation
- Clinical deterioration recognition

NCLEX-RN:
- Safety and priority
- Delegation
- Acute complication recognition
- Pharmacology awareness (not prescribing)

NP:
- Pathophysiologic mechanisms
- Diagnostic criteria thresholds
- Prescribing logic
- Contraindications
- Referral timing
- Risk stratification
- Multi-system interplay

=====================================
QUALITY CONTROL RULES
=====================================

- Do not fabricate drug strengths or nonexistent criteria.
- Use real, guideline-supported diagnostic thresholds.
- Clearly distinguish between scope levels.
- Avoid overcomplication at RPN level.
- Avoid superficial simplification at NP level.
- Maintain clinical accuracy.
- Ensure red flag physiology is explained (not just listed).
