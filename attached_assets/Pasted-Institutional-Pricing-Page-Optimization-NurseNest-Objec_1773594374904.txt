Institutional Pricing Page Optimization – NurseNest

Objective
Improve the institutional pricing section of the NurseNest website so that it reflects best practices used by successful EdTech SaaS platforms (e.g., UWorld, Kaplan, Archer, ATI). The goal is to make the pricing easier for schools to understand, eliminate seat-range confusion, add enterprise-level credibility, and increase demo/contact conversions without breaking existing styling or page layout.

General Requirements

* Do not break the existing site layout or theme.
* Maintain the current NurseNest visual style.
* Keep the three-card layout but improve the logic of the seat ranges and messaging.
* Add one additional enterprise tier option.
* Add clear call-to-action buttons for schools.
* Ensure this section appears professional and credible to institutional buyers.
* Keep everything responsive for desktop and mobile.

Pricing Structure Improvements

Update the seat tiers to remove the current gap between 100 and 250 seats.

Current structure has a problem because many nursing programs have between 120–200 students.

Implement this seat structure:

Small Program
Up to 50 seats
$8 / seat / month
Example: $400/month for 50 students

Medium Program (Most Popular)
51–150 seats
$6 / seat / month
Example: $900/month for 150 students

Large Program
151–300 seats
$4 / seat / month
Example: $1,200/month for 300 students

Enterprise Program
300+ seats
Custom institutional pricing
Add text: “Contact our team for a custom institutional package.”

Make sure the pricing still visually shows per-seat cost and an example monthly total.

Add one outcomes-focused statement under the description:

Programs using NurseNest strengthen clinical reasoning, exam readiness, and student performance through adaptive practice and analytics.

Design Improvements

Keep the three-column card layout but add the Enterprise card if space allows.

Visual requirements:

* Maintain the “Most Popular” badge on the Medium tier.
* Keep icons consistent with the NurseNest style.
* Ensure all text spacing and alignment are balanced.
* Ensure the pricing numbers remain the most visually prominent element.

Responsiveness

Verify that:

* Pricing cards stack correctly on tablet and mobile.
* CTA buttons remain visible without scrolling excessively.
* Seat descriptions and pricing remain readable on small screens.

Conversion Optimization

Add a small trust section under the pricing cards:

Why Nursing Programs Choose NurseNest

* Built specifically for REx-PN, NCLEX-RN, and NP preparation
* Adaptive question bank with detailed rationales
* Instructor dashboards for tracking student readiness
* Analytics to identify knowledge gaps across cohorts
* Continuous content updates aligned with exam blueprints

Technical Safeguards

Ensure that:

* Pricing values can be easily edited from configuration or CMS.
* Seat ranges do not overlap.
* Enterprise tier uses a contact workflow instead of checkout.
* CTA buttons connect to the correct contact or demo request form.
* Analytics events track demo requests and institutional interest.

Acceptance Criteria

Before completing implementation verify that:

* Seat tiers now cover all ranges with no gaps.
* An Enterprise tier exists for 300+ students.
* CTA buttons exist on every pricing card.
* Institutional messaging highlights outcomes and exam preparation.
* Minimum seat and annual billing notes are visible.
* LMS integration messaging is included.
* Layout remains consistent with the NurseNest theme.
* Page works correctly on desktop, tablet, and mobile.
* No existing navigation or tier routing is broken.

Goal

The final institutional pricing section should look credible to nursing schools, remove confusion about seat ranges, and encourage institutions to request demos or contact NurseNest for access.
