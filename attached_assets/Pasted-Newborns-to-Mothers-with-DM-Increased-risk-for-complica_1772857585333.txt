Newborns to Mothers with DM
-	Increased risk for complications after birth (hypoglycemia)
-	Also at risk for hypocalcemia, hyperbilirubinemia, and respiratory distress syndrome
-	During intrauterine life, exposure to elevated maternal glucose can cause the fetus to produce high levels of insulin
-	After birth, the newborn loses the maternal supply of glucose but continues to produce high levels of insulin
-	Symptoms of hypoglycemia: jitteriness, irritability, hypotonia, apnea, lethargy, temperature instability.
-	Immediate intervention is required to prevent neurologic damage

Carotid Endarterectomy
-	Removes atherosclerotic plaque from the carotid artery 
-	Clients with carotid artery disease are at increased risk for transient ischemic attack and sroke
-	Post surgical risks include cerebral ischemia and infarction as well as bleeding
-	Blood pressure is closely monitored during the first 24 hours post surgery which can cause hemorrhage or airway obstruction 
-	Systolic blood pressure is maintained at 100-150 mm Hg to ensure adequate cerebral perfusion and avoidance of hemorrhage or strain 

Peripheral Artery Disease
-	Chronic, atherosclerotic disease caused by buildup of plaque within the arteries
-	Commonly affects the lower extremities and can lead to tissue necrosis
-	Home management:
o	Lower the extremities below the heart when sitting and lying down – improves arterial flow
o	Engage in moderate exercise (30-40 min walk twice daily) promotes collateral circulation and distal tissue perfusion
o	Perform daily skin care including application of lotion – prevents skin breakdown from dry skin
o	Maintain mild warmth 
o	Stop smoking (prevents vessel spasm and constriction)
o	Avoid tight clothing and stress (vasoconstriction)
o	Take prescribed medications – increased blood flow and prevents blood clot development 
-	Signs and symptoms:
o	Intermittent claudication (ischemic muscle pain)
o	Decreased peripheral pulses 
o	Hair loss
o	Thick, brittle nails
o	Ulcers
o	Gangrene
o	Cool, dry, shiny skin

Oxytocin 
-	High alert medication used for labor induction or augmentation
-	Evaluate fetal heart rate and uterine contraction pattern every 15 minutes during first stage of labor and every 5 minutes during the second stage
-	Continuous fetal heart rate monitoring, non-intermittent auscultation, is necessary until the infusion rate of oxytocin is stable
-	Should be administered via an electronic infusion pump which provides accurate dosing and prevents maternal hypotension associated with rapid oxytocin bolus
-	Nurse should monitor intake and output to identify fluid retention which precedes water intoxication
-	Potential adverse reaction is dilutional hyponatremia, convulsions, death

Endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)
-	Upper scope used to assess the pancreatic and biliary ducts
-	Using fluoroscopy with contrast media, the ducts can be visualized and treatments, including removal of obstructions, dilation of strictures, and biopsies can be performed
-	Perforation or irritation of these areas during the procedure can cause acute pancreatitis, a potentially life-threatening complication 
o	signs = acute epigastric or LUQ pain, often radiating to the back, and a rapid rise in pancreatic enzymes (amylase, lipase)

Digoxin
-	cardiac glycoside that increases cardiac contractility but slows the heart rate and conduction
-	used in heart failure to increase cardiac output and atrial fibrillation 
-	drug is excreted almost exclusively by the kidney 
-	BUN and creatinine levels are measurements of kidney function
-	Normal range is 0.6-1.3 mg/dl 
-	Elderly clients tend to develop age-related decrease in GFR
-	These clients and those with obvious kidney injury can accumulate digoxin
-	Early symptoms of toxicity: nausea and vomiting 
-	Later signs toxicity: arrhythmias, including heart blocks

Vasectomy
-	Vasa deferens (ducts that carry sperm from the testicles to the urethra)
-	Are cut and sealed, preventing sperm from entering the ejaculation
-	Vasa deferentia are severed in the scrotum at the site before the seminal vesicles and prostate
-	Following a vasectomy, sperm continue to be produced but are absorbed by the body 
-	Alternative birth control should be used until the health care provider confirms that semen samples taken at a follow up appointment are free of sperm otherwise pregnancy can occur

Encopresis 
Involuntary passage of stools in children over 4
A common cause is functional constipation
-	Management:
o	Foot stool
o	Scheduling regular toilet sitting time after meals to restore toileting habits 
o	Keeping a record log of toilet sitting times and soiling episodes to evaluate the success of the treatment plan
o	Gradually taper down the use of stool softeners to prevent rebound constipation

Heimlich on Babies
-	Position baby face down with head lower than body and support baby’s head with forearm against thigh
-	Provide 5 back slaps between shoulder blades with heel of hand
-	Turn baby face up if still choking and give 5 chest thrusts on sternum with 2-3 fingers
-	Repeat 5 back slaps and 5 chest thrusts until the object dislodges or perform CPR if the baby becomes unconscious

Abnormal Neurological Assessment
-	Nuchal rigidity
-	New onset unilateral drift of a limb
-	Pupils < 3mm or >5 mm in diameter; absent oculocephalic reflex
-	Presence of Babinski reflex in an adult


Alcohol Withdrawal 
-	Abrupt cessation causes CNS hyperactivity that can result in seizures and death
-	Delirium tremens, which occurs 48-96 hours following the last drink, is characterized by agitation, hypertension, diaphoresis, hallucinations, and tachycardia 
-	Withdrawal symptoms can be prevented with benzodiazepines 

Diverticulosis
-	Saclike protrusions develop in the large intestine caused by increased intraabdominal pressure and or chronic constipation 
-	When the diverticula become inflamed, the individual has diverticulitis
-	Acute care for diverticulitis focuses on allowing the colon to rest and the inflammation ot resolve 
o	NPO status: rest of the bowel
o	IV fluids to prevent dehydration when NPO
o	Pain relief with IV medications 
o	Preventing increased intrabdominal pressure 
o	Preventing increased intestinal motility
o	Promote a diet high in fiber 
o	Drink a lot of water
o	Fiber supplement such as psyllium or bran may be advised
