Late decelerations/variable heart rate nursing
- Administer IV fluid bolus
- Reposition client laterally
- Discontinue IV oxytocin infusion
- Place internal fetal scalp electrode
- Prepare for cesarean birth if interventions don’t work
Maximizing fetal oxygenation during pushing with variable decelerations
- Push in lateral position to relieve pressure on the maternal inferior vena cava and
optimize uteroplacental blood flow
- Use the open glottis pushing technique (slow exhalation while pushing) to promote
maternal cardiac output and uteroplacental perfusion unlike closed glottis pushing
- Push every other contraction
- Shorter pushes 6-8 seconds promote fetal oxygenation
Perinatal Asphyxia
- Caused by inadequate fetal oxygenation and can lead to poor cognitive outcomes of the
newborn (learning disabilities, hypoxic ischemic encephalopathy, cerebral palsy)
- Newborn assessment findings associated with perinatal asphyxia includes
o Low apgar scores less than 7 at 5 mins may indicate poor transition to
extrauterine life due to perinatal asphyxia
o Neonatal seizures- jerking movements that do not go away and oxular
manifestations (rapid eye movement, excessive blinking) are findings that
require further evaluation to determine if seizure activity is present because
hypoxia induced brain damage may be the underlying cause
o Acidemia: umbilical cord arterial blood can be analyzed to evaluate fetal
oxygenation status in utero. A low arterial blood pH is caused by a lack of oxygen
and lactic acid accumulation

Pavlik Harness
- Puts patients hips in a slightly flexed and abducted position
- Worn for 3-5 months until the hip joint is stable

- Teaching: dress client in shirt and knee socks to protect the skin before applying the
straps, regularly assess the skin under the straps for redness or breakdown, apply the
straps of the harness over the clients diaper to keep the harness clean and dry. Do not
apply lotion or powder and keep the harness on at all times even diaper changes
Negative Pressure Wound Therapy
- Encourages healing by removing fluid, exudate, infectious organisms, and encourages
circulation of blood to the wound bed
- Sterile foam dressing is cut to fit the wound, is placed on the wound bed, and then
covered with an occlusive dressing to create a seal.
- Requires routine dressing changes the ensure appropriate device function and to allow
evaluation of wound healing
- Administer prophylactic analgesics to prevent discomfort, apply a skin protectant to
intact skin around the wound to promote an air tight seal when the occlusive film
dressing is placed and suction applied
- Cut the foam dressing to the size and shape of the wound to ensure close contact with
the wound bed and a strong seal suction when the vaccume assisted closure unit is
connected and turned on
- Apply the prescribed negative pressure and observe for compression of the foam
dressing
- Ensure no air leaks
Rhonchi
- Continuous, low pitched wheezes head on expiration that sound like moaning or snoring
and originates from bronchi filled with thick mucus secretions or partial blockage
- Expected find in bronchitis due to infection and inflammation of the upper airways
Oxybutynin
- Anticholinergic that is frequently used to treat overactive bladder
- Side effects: new onset constipation, dry mouth, flushing, heat intolerance, blurred
vision, drowsiness
- Decreased sweat production may lead to hyperthermia
- Increase fluids and bulk forming foods and avoid heat
Acute Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction
- Caused by transfusion of mismatched blood
- Results in antibody mediated hypersensitivity reaction that can be fatal.
- Clients have fever with chills, flank/back pain, and dark red urine as well as tachycardia
and hypotension due to inflammatory cytokine release shortly following a transfusion.
Angioedema and wheezing are not seen
- Positive direct coombs test (blood test for antibodies)
- Hemolysis, increased LDH and increased indirect bilirubin
- Complications: AKI, DIC

Anaphylactic transfusion reaction
- Occurs seconds to minutes after initiation of transfusion due to massive histamine
release
- Symptoms include respiratory distress (dyspnea, wheezing), skin changes, hives,
flushing, abdominal pain, angioedema, and hypotension. Fever with chills is not
common
Febrile nonhemolytic transfusion reaction
- Most common
- Benign occurrence caused by cytokine release from leukocytes within the donor blood
product. Clinical manifestations include fever, chills, and malaise within 1-6 hours of
transfusion initiation
- No skin changes, hypotension, angioedema, or dark red urine
Levetiracetam
- Anticonvulsant medication that can be used to prevent and treat seizures in clients with
brain tumors
- Causes minimal medication interactions compared to other anticonvulsants and is a
preferred antieleptic medication
Short acting insulin
- Reach peak effect 1.5-5 hours after administration peak
Tumor Lysis Syndrome
- Oncologic emergency that occurs when cancer treatment destroys a large number of
cancer cells resulting in a massive release of intracellular components (potassium
phosphate and nucleic acids) into circulation
- Nucleic acids overwhelm the kidneys leading to hyperuricemia and aki from uric acid
crystal formation
- TLS prophylaxis includes hypouricemic medications to dissolve and prevent urate crystal
formation that contributes to aki
- A serum uric acid level within the normal range indicates education effectiveness
Diabetes Insipidus
- Results in low levels of ADH which is produced by the hypothalamus and stored in the
pituitary gland. The function of ADH is to concentrate urine by signaling the kidneys to
retain water in the setting of thirst. When adh levels are insufficient the kidneys excrete
large quantities of very dilute urine which causes hypernatremia and increased serum
osmolality which leads to excessive thirst
- Use of desmopressin in clients with DI will lower urinary output and cause the urine
specific gravity to increase
