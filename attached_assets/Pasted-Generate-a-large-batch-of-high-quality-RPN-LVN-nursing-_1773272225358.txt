EXAMPLE QUESTION STRUCTURE

Question:

A nurse is caring for a client with chronic kidney disease. Which finding indicates worsening renal function?

A Decreased serum creatinine  
B Increased urine output  
C Elevated serum potassium  
D Decreased blood urea nitrogen

Correct Answer: C

Correct Answer Explanation:
Elevated potassium occurs when the kidneys cannot adequately excrete potassium, which is common in worsening renal failure.

Incorrect Options:

A: Creatinine usually increases rather than decreases in renal failure.

B: Urine output typically decreases in worsening kidney failure.

D: Blood urea nitrogen increases rather than decreases when renal function declines.

Clinical Takeaway:
Hyperkalemia is a common and potentially life-threatening complication of renal failure.

Exam Pearl:
Monitor potassium levels closely in patients with renal dysfunction.
