TASK: CLEAN AND STANDARDIZE LESSON TITLES + REMOVE TIER LABELS FROM UI

You are working inside the NurseNest production platform.

Your task is to clean all lesson titles and content so users never see tier labels such as RPN, RN, or NP inside the lesson interface.

Tier classification must remain in the database but should never appear in the visible lesson text or titles.

---

OBJECTIVES

1. Remove tier words from lesson titles
2. Remove tier references inside lesson content
3. Shorten excessively long lesson titles
4. Preserve tier assignment in the database
5. Maintain SEO-friendly medical titles
6. Do not modify lesson difficulty or structure

---

STEP 1 — REMOVE TIER LABELS FROM TITLES

Scan the lessons table for titles containing:

RPN
RN
NP
NCLEX
LVN
Nurse Practitioner

Examples to fix:

Before:
RN Pulmonary Embolism
NP Advanced Heart Failure
RPN Diabetes Mellitus
NCLEX Stroke Management

After:
Pulmonary Embolism
Heart Failure
Diabetes Mellitus
Stroke

RULE:
Titles must only contain the medical topic name.

Tier information must remain stored in:

lesson_tier field

but never appear in:

lesson_title
lesson_header
lesson_display_title

---

STEP 2 — SHORTEN LONG TITLES

Some lesson titles are too long and break the UI.

Shorten titles to 2–4 words whenever possible.

Examples:

Before:
Pathophysiology and Management of Acute Pulmonary Embolism

After:
Pulmonary Embolism

Before:
Advanced Pharmacologic Treatment of Heart Failure

After:
Heart Failure Treatment

Before:
Understanding the Pathophysiology of Cardiac Tamponade

After:
Cardiac Tamponade

Rules:

• Max title length: 45 characters
• Prefer concise medical terminology
• Use standard disease names

---

STEP 3 — REMOVE TIER REFERENCES INSIDE LESSONS

Scan lesson body text and remove phrases like:

"As an RN you should..."
"For NP practice..."
"In NCLEX exams..."
"RPN nurses should monitor..."

Replace with neutral phrasing.

Examples:

Before:
RN nurses must monitor potassium levels carefully.

After:
Potassium levels must be monitored carefully.

Before:
NP providers should prescribe ACE inhibitors.

After:
ACE inhibitors are commonly prescribed.

---

STEP 4 — KEEP TIER CONTENT INTERNALLY

Difficulty differences should remain, but without mentioning the tier.

Example:

RN lesson:

NP lesson:

Users should simply see:

Lesson title
Lesson content

They should NOT see:

RN
NP
RPN
NCLEX

STEP 5 — VALIDATE TITLES

Before saving a lesson:

Check:

Title length < 45 characters
Title contains only the condition/topic
No tier terms present
No extra phrases like "lesson" or "overview"

Examples of correct titles:

Pulmonary Embolism
Cardiac Tamponade
Torsades de Pointes
