The current flashcards landing page feels too busy, too long, too promotional, and too scattered. It currently looks more like a generic website landing page than a polished exam-prep interface. I want it redesigned so it feels more like a serious study platform students trust immediately.

CURRENT PROBLEMS TO FIX

1. Too much clutter above the fold
- There are too many badges, stats, chips, buttons, and sections competing for attention.
- The page does not guide the eye well.
- The topic list is overwhelming and visually noisy.

2. It feels too much like a marketing homepage
- Sections like “Why NurseNest Flashcards,” comparison tables, repeated feature blocks, and repeated navigation-style blocks make the page feel busy.
- I want this to feel more like a student dashboard / study launcher.

3. Weak visual hierarchy
- The important action should be starting a flashcard session.
- Right now too many things have similar visual weight.
- The page should guide users quickly toward choosing topic, mode, and starting.

4. The layout does not feel premium enough
- It needs more polish, spacing, restraint, and intentional structure.
- It should feel more clinically professional and less like a template.

5. Too many topic chips shown at once
- The huge wall of topics is visually exhausting.
- It needs better organization, collapsing, grouping, or searchable filtering.

- calm
