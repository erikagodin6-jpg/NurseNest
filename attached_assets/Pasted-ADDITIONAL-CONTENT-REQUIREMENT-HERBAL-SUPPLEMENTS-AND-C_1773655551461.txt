ADDITIONAL CONTENT REQUIREMENT
HERBAL SUPPLEMENTS AND COMPLEMENTARY MEDICINE INTERACTIONS

Many nursing licensing exams (REx-PN, NCLEX-RN, NP exams) include questions about herbal supplements, naturopathic remedies, and complementary therapies that may interact with medications.

Expand the NurseNest pharmacology and patient-safety content to include a structured Herbal & Complementary Medicine Safety module.

This content should appear inside pharmacology learning sections and also be integrated into question banks.

PRIMARY GOAL

Teach learners how herbal products and naturopathic therapies interact with medications, affect physiology, and influence nursing assessment, documentation, and patient teaching.

Do not frame this as alternative medicine promotion.
Frame it as patient safety and medication-interaction awareness.

HERBAL MEDICINE HUB PAGE

Create a learning module called:

Herbal Supplements and Medication Interactions

Include sections for:

• Why nurses must ask about supplements

• Bleeding risk herbs

• Blood pressure–affecting supplements
• Blood sugar–altering supplements

EXAMPLE INTERACTION THEMES

The system should include content and questions around concepts such as:

• St. John’s Wort reducing effectiveness of certain medications via enzyme induction
• Ginkgo increasing bleeding risk with anticoagulants
• Garlic supplements increasing bleeding risk
• Ginseng altering blood glucose
• Valerian or kava increasing sedation with CNS depressants
• Echinacea affecting immune function
• Melatonin affecting sleep cycles and interacting with sedatives
• Turmeric affecting anticoagulation pathways
• Herbal supplements altering liver enzyme metabolism

Again: do not copy text from external sources.

QUESTION BANK INTEGRATION

Add herbal supplement questions into:

Pharmacology question banks  
Medication safety sections  
Patient teaching scenarios  
Pre-operative assessment scenarios  
Drug interaction questions

Examples of question themes:

• A patient taking warfarin also takes ginkgo — what should the nurse monitor?
• A patient reports St. John’s Wort use before starting antidepressants — what teaching is needed?
• A patient scheduled for surgery reports taking garlic supplements — what should the nurse do?
• Which herbal supplement may increase bleeding risk?
• Which supplement may reduce effectiveness of certain medications?

These questions should include detailed rationales explaining:

• why the correct answer is correct
• why the other options are incorrect
• the patient safety principle being tested

PATIENT ASSESSMENT AND DOCUMENTATION

Add nursing assessment teaching such as:

• always ask patients about herbal supplements
• document supplements in medication history
• recognize that many patients do not consider supplements to be medications
• assess for potential interactions
• educate patients about safety concerns

Include sample patient-teaching statements nurses might provide.

SURGERY AND ANESTHESIA SAFETY

Create a section explaining that certain herbal supplements should be discontinued prior to surgery due to bleeding or sedation risk.

Use this concept in exam-style questions.
